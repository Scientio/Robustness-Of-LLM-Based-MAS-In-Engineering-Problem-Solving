##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \( f \) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \( L \) = length of the pipe (m)
- \( D \) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \( \rho \) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \( v \) = flow velocity (m/s)

For water, the density \( \rho \) is approximately 1000 kg/m³. 

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number \( Re \) to find the friction factor \( f \):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water at room temperature is about \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa} \cdot \text{s} \):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

For \( Re < 2000 \), the flow is laminar, and the friction factor \( f \) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

So,

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now substituting values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Preliminary Decision: The pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa.
