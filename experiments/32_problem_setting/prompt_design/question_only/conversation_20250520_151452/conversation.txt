##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/mÂ³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. **Determine the flow regime**: Calculate the Reynolds number (\(Re\)):
   
\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature (\(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\) and \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\)):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

2. **Determine the friction factor \(f\)**: For \(Re < 2000\), we can use the laminar flow equation:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. **Calculate the pressure loss**:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating the components:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

**Preliminary Decision: The pressure loss in the pipe is approximately 0.32 Pa.**
