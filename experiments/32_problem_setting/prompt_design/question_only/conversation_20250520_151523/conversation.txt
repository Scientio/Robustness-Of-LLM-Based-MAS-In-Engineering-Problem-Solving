##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. **Determine the flow regime**: Calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to find the flow regime.

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at 20°C:
- \(\rho \approx 1000 \, kg/m³\)
- \(\mu \approx 1 \times 10^{-3} \, Pa \cdot s\)

Calculating Re:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

2. **Friction factor**: For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\).

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. **Calculate pressure loss**:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, Pa
\]

Preliminary Decision: The pressure loss in the pipe is approximately 0.32 Pa.
