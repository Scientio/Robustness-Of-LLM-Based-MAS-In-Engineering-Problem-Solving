##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³), for water approximately 1000 kg/m³
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (\(Re\)) to find the friction factor (\(f\)):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming \(\mu\) (dynamic viscosity of water) is approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

For \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar, and the friction factor can be calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now, substituting the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating further:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Preliminary Decision: The pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa.
