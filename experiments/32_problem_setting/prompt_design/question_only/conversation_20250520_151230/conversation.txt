##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number (Re)** to determine the friction factor:
   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]
   For water at room temperature, \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\) and \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\).

2. **Assuming laminar flow** (Re < 2000) for this low velocity:
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re}
   \]

3. **Calculate the pressure loss** using the values provided.

Let's assume \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\) and calculate:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Thus, for laminar flow:
\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now, substituting into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Preliminary Decision: 0.32 Pa
