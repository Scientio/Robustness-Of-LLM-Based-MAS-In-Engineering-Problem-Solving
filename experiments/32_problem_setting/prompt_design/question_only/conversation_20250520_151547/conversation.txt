##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³) (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (\(Re < 2000\)), \(f\) can be calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where the Reynolds number \(Re\) is given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) for water of approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\):

1. Calculate \(Re\):
\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re < 2000\), we can use the laminar flow equation for \(f\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

2. Now, substitute the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Preliminary Decision: The pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa.
