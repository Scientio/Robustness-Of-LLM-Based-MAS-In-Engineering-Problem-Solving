##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/mÂ³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

For water, \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\).

To find the friction factor \(f\), we need the Reynolds number \(Re\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming \(\mu\) (dynamic viscosity of water) is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

For \(Re < 2000\), flow is laminar, and the friction factor \(f\) can be calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now substituting the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]
