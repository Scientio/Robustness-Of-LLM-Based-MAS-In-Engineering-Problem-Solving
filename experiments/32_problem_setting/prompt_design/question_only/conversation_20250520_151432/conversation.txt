##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

For water, the density \(\rho\) is approximately 1000 kg/m³. 

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number (Re):**
   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]
   Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\):
   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
   \]

2. **Determine the friction factor (f):**
   For a Reynolds number of 1000, the flow is likely laminar, and the friction factor can be estimated using:
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

3. **Calculate the pressure loss (\(\Delta P\)):**
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 5 = 32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

Preliminary Decision: The pressure loss in the pipe is 32 Pa.
