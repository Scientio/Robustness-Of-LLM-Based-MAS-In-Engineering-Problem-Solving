To approach this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which states that the pressure loss (ΔP) is given by:

ΔP = f * (L/D) * (ρ * v² / 2)

Where:
- f = friction factor (depends on the flow regime)
- L = length of the pipe
- D = diameter of the pipe
- ρ = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- v = flow velocity

First, we need to determine the flow regime (laminar or turbulent) to find the friction factor. Given the low velocity, we might expect laminar flow. 

I suggest we calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to confirm the flow type:

Re = (ρ * v * D) / μ

Where μ is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately 0.001 Pa·s).

Once we have Re, we can find f.
