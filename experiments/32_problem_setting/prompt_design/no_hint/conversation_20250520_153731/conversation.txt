##### LEAD:
Team, we need to determine the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water is flowing at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which states:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor (which we need to determine),
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

We need to find the friction factor \(f\), which can depend on the flow regime (laminar or turbulent). Given the low velocity, it’s likely laminar, and we can use:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming \(\mu\) (dynamic viscosity of water) is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\).

Let's calculate \(Re\) first, then find \(f\) and finally, \(\Delta P\).

What are your thoughts on this approach?
##### ADVISOR:

   \[

   \[
   f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025

   \[
