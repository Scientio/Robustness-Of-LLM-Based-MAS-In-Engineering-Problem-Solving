\[

- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (approximately 1000 kg/m³ for water),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is around \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number and then find the friction factor. After that, we can calculate the pressure loss.
