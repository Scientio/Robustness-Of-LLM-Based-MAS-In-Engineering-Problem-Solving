##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. **Determine the Reynolds number (Re)** to find the friction factor:
\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]
Assuming water at room temperature, \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\).

2. **Calculate Re**:
\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

3. **Determine the friction factor (f)**:
For \(Re = 1000\), we can use the Moody chart or empirical correlations. For laminar flow (\(Re < 2000\)), \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\):
\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

4. **Substitute values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation**:
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Preliminary Decision: The pressure loss in the pipe is approximately 0.32 Pa.
