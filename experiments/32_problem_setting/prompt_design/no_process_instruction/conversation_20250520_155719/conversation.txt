##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number (Re)** to determine the flow regime and friction factor:
   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]
   Assuming water at room temperature, \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\) and dynamic viscosity \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\).

2. **Use the flow velocity** \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\) and diameter \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\):
   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
   \]

3. **Determine the friction factor \(f\)**. For \(Re < 2000\), flow is laminar and:
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

4. **Substitute values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation**:
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
   = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]
   \[
   = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

Preliminary Decision: The pressure loss in the pipe is approximately 0.32 Pa.
