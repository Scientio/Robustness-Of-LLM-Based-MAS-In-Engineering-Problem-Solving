##### LEAD:
To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number (Re)** to find the friction factor:
\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]
Assuming the water density (\(\rho\)) is approximately 1000 kg/m³ and the dynamic viscosity (\(\mu\)) of water is about \(1 \times 10^{-3}\) Pa·s, we can substitute the values:
- \(D = 0.1\) m
- \(v = 0.01\) m/s

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

2. **Determine the friction factor (f)**:
For a Reynolds number of 1000, the flow is likely in the laminar regime. The friction factor for laminar flow is given by:
\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]
Thus:
\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. **Calculate pressure loss (\(\Delta P\))**:
Now we can plug in all the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Preliminary Decision: The pressure loss in the pipe is approximately 0.32 Pa.
