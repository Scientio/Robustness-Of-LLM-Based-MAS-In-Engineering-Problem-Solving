##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \( f \) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \( L \) = length of the pipe (m)
- \( D \) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \( \rho \) = density of the fluid (kg/mÂ³)
- \( v \) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number (Re)** to find the friction factor:
\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]
Assuming water at room temperature:
- \( \rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)
- \( \mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \)

2. **Calculate Re**:
\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

3. **Determine the friction factor (f)** using the Moody chart or empirical correlations. For \( Re = 1000 \) in a smooth pipe, \( f \) is approximately 0.02.

4. **Calculate pressure loss**:
\[
\Delta P = 0.02 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.02 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.02 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.1 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Preliminary Decision: The pressure loss in the pipe is approximately 0.1 Pa.
