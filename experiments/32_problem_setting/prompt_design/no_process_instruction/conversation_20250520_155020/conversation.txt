##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³) (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number (Re)** to determine the flow regime:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity (\(\mu\)) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

2. **Determine the friction factor (f)**. For a Reynolds number of 1000 in a smooth pipe, we can use the following approximation for laminar flow:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. **Substituting values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation**:

- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating the pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Preliminary Decision: Pressure loss is approximately 0.32 Pa.
