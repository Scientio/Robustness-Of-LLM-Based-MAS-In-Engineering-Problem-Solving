To calculate the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \( f \) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \( L \) = length of the pipe (m)
- \( D \) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \( \rho \) = density of the fluid (kg/mÂ³)
- \( v \) = flow velocity (m/s)

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (Re) to find the friction factor \( f \):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature:
- \( \rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)
- \( \mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \)

Now substituting the values:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
