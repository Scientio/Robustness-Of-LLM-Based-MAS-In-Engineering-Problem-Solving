##### LEAD:
To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe (10 m),
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1 m),
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \( f \):

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water at room temperature is about \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \), we can plug in our values.

Next, we'll determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent based on the Reynolds number. For laminar flow (\( Re < 2000 \)), the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[ 
f = \frac{64}{Re} 
\]

For turbulent flow (\( Re > 4000 \)), we would need to use empirical correlations or charts like the Moody chart.
