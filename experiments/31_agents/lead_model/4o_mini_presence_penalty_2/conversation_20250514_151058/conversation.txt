##### LEAD:
To address the problem of pressure loss in a pipe with given dimensions and flow conditions, we need to apply the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10 m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1 m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (\(Re\)) to find the appropriate friction factor (\(f\)). The Reynolds number is calculated as follows:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity (\(\mu\)) of water at room temperature is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\), we can calculate \(Re\):

Now let's calculate \(Re\):
1. \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
2. \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
3. \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)

Calculating \(Re\):
\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

With \(Re = 1000\), we are in the laminar flow regime where the friction factor can be determined using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now we can substitute values back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

1. \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
2. \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
3. \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
4. \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

Calculating the pressure loss:
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

In summary, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately 0.32 Pa.
