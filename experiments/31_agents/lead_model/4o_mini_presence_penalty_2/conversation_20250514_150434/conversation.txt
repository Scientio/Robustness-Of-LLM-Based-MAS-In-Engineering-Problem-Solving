To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to find the appropriate friction factor \(f\). The formula for Reynolds number is:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity (\(\mu\)) of water at room temperature is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\), let's compute Re and subsequently determine the friction factor using the Moody chart or an empirical correlation.
