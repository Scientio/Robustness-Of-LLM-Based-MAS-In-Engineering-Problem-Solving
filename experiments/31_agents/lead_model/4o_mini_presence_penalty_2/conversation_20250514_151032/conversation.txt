\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to find the Reynolds number to determine the friction factor \( f \):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is about \( 1 \times 10^{-3} \) Pa·s, we can calculate \( Re \). If \( Re < 2000 \), the flow is laminar, and we can use:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

If \( Re > 4000 \), we would look up \( f \) from the Moody chart.
