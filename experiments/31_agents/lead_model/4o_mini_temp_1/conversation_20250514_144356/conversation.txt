To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m続)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

We need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For flow in a circular pipe, the friction factor can be calculated using the Moody chart or approximated using empirical formulas such as the Colebrook equation for turbulent flow or the Hagen-Poiseuille equation for laminar flow.
