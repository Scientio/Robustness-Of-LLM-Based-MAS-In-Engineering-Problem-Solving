To determine the pressure loss, we need to use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to determine the flow regime and subsequently find the friction factor \(f\). The Reynolds number is given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) (dynamic viscosity) for water is about \(1 \times 10^{-3} \text{Pa·s}\). 

Let’s calculate \(Re\) first, and then we will discuss how to find the friction factor for either laminar or turbulent flow.
