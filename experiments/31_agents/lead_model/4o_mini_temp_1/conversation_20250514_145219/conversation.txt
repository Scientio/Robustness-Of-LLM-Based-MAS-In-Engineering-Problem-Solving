To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which states that:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

We need to focus on finding the friction factor \(f\). Since our flow velocity is quite low (0.01 m/s), we suspect that we may be dealing with laminar flow. In that case, the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} \]

Where the Reynolds number \(Re\) is calculated as:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

With \(\mu\) being the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \text{ Pa}\cdot\text{s}\) at room temperature).
