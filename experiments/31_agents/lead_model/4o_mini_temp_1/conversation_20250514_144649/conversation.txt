Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1 m and a length of 10 m, through which water flows at a velocity of 0.01 m/s. To find the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \frac{L}{D} \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the velocity of water.

We need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Given that the flow velocity is low, we should consider that this might be laminar flow, allowing us to use the formula for the friction factor for laminar flow:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Here, \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)).

Let's calculate \(Re\) first, and from there, determine \(f\) and then the pressure loss. What do you all think? Any concerns or alternative suggestions?
