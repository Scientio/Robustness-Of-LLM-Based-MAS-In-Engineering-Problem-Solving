- Flow Velocity (v): 0.01 m/s

To solve this, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³).

We need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water around \(0.001 \, \text{Pa}\cdot\text{s}\):

Let's first calculate the Reynolds number, and from there, derive the friction factor \(f\). 

Could you please assist with the calculations and verify if the flow is laminar or turbulent?
