To find the pressure loss in a pipe due to friction, we'll use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the pipe length (10 m),
- \(D\) is the pipe diameter (0.1 m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

To determine \(f\), we need to assess whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. The Reynolds number \(Re\) will help us:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water's dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar. The friction factor for laminar flow can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]
