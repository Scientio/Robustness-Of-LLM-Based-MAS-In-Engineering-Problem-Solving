We have a pipe with diameter D = 0.1 m, length L = 10 m, and water flowing at a velocity V = 0.01 m/s. The pressure drop ΔP due to friction losses is calculated with the Darcy–Weisbach equation:

  ΔP = f · (L/D) · (ρ · V²/2)

For water, we assume ρ = 1000 kg/m³. The friction factor f is critical here. With our very low velocity and small pipe, the Reynolds number is:

  Re = (ρ · V · D) / μ
Assuming μ = 0.001 Pa·s (water), then
  Re = (1000 × 0.01 × 0.1) / 0.001 = 1

Since Re is 1, the flow is in the laminar regime, and the friction factor is given by:

  f = 64/Re = 64/1 = 64

Plugging in:
  ΔP = 64 × (10/0.1) × [1000 × (0.01)²/2]
  = 64 × 100 × [1000 × 1e-4/2]
  = 6400 × (0.05)
  = 320 Pa
