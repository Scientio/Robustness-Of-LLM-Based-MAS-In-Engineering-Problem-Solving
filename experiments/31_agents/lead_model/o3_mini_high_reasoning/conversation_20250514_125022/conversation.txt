We need to estimate the pressure loss in a pipe 10 m long with a 0.1 m diameter carrying water at 0.01 m/s. We use the Darcy–Weisbach equation:

  ΔP = f · (L/D) · (ρ·v²/2)

Step 1: Determine the flow regime. For water (ρ ≈ 1000 kg/m³, μ ≈ 1×10⁻³ Pa·s):
  Re = (ρ·v·D)/μ = (1000·0.01·0.1)/(1×10⁻³) = 1000
Since Re = 1000 < 2000, the flow is laminar.

Step 2: Compute the friction factor. In laminar flow,
  f = 64/Re = 64/1000 = 0.064

Step 3: Calculate the pressure loss:
  L/D = 10/0.1 = 100
  Dynamic term, (ρ·v²/2) = (1000·(0.01)²/2) = (1000·0.0001/2) = 0.05 Pa
So, ΔP = 0.064 · 100 · 0.05 = 0.32 Pa

Note the importance of correctly choosing f. In laminar flow, the simple f = 64/Re is valid. Using turbulent formulas (like Colebrook–White) here would be misleading.
