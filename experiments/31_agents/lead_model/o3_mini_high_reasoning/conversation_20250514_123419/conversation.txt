##### LEAD:
Team, we need to determine the pressure drop due to friction in a 10 m long pipe of 0.1 m diameter with water flowing at 0.01 m/s.

Step 1. Calculate the Reynolds number:
 • Assume water has ρ = 1000 kg/m³ and μ = 1×10⁻³ Pa·s.
 • Re = (ρ × V × D) / μ = (1000 × 0.01 × 0.1) / (1×10⁻³) = 1000.
Since Re = 1000 (<2000), the flow is laminar.

Step 2. Determine the friction factor f:
 • For laminar flow, f = 64 / Re = 64 / 1000 = 0.064.
Note that for turbulent flow we’d need to use a different (Colebrook/Moody) approach, but here the laminar assumption holds.

Step 3. Use the Darcy–Weisbach equation for pressure loss:
  ΔP = f × (L / D) × (ρ × V² / 2)
Substitute the values:
 • L / D = 10 / 0.1 = 100,
 • Dynamic pressure = (1000 × (0.01)² / 2) = (1000 × 0.0001 / 2) = 0.05 Pa,
thus,
  ΔP = 0.064 × 100 × 0.05 = 0.32 Pa.

Our suggestion is to use the laminar flow friction factor (f = 64/Re) and the Darcy–Weisbach equation as shown.

Team, do you agree with this approach? Are there any considerations (such as roughness effects or additional losses) we might need to explore, even if they would be minor at such low velocities? 

Preliminary Decision: The pressure loss is approximately 0.32 Pa.
