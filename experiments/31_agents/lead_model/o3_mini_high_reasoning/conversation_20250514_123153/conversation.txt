• Step 1: Compute the Reynolds number:
  Re = (v·D)/ν.
Assuming water’s kinematic viscosity ν ≈ 1×10⁻⁶ m²/s, we get:
  Re = (0.01 m/s × 0.1 m) / (1×10⁻⁶ m²/s) = 1000.
Since Re < 2300, the flow is laminar.

• Step 2: Determine the friction factor f:
  For laminar flow in a circular pipe, we use f = 64/Re.
Thus, f = 64/1000 ≈ 0.064.
(Note: For turbulent flow, we’d have to use the Colebrook or similar formulations where roughness plays a significant role, but here the laminar regime simplifies things.)

• Step 3: Calculate the pressure drop using the Darcy–Weisbach equation:
  Δp = f · (L/D) · (ρ·v²/2).

  v² = (0.01 m/s)² = 0.0001 m²/s²,
  ρ·v²/2 = 1000 × 0.0001/2 = 0.05 Pa.
Now, Δp = 0.064 × 100 × 0.05 = 0.32 Pa.
