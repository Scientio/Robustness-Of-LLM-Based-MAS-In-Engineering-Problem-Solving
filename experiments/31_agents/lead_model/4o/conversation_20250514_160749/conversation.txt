\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (\( \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

The friction factor \( f \) depends on the Reynolds number \( Re \), given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (\( \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \)).

For laminar flow (\( Re < 2000 \)), \( f \) can be calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]
