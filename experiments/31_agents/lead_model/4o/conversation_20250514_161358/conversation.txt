\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:  
- \( \Delta P \) = pressure loss  
- \( f \) = friction factor  
- \( L \) = pipe length = 10 m  
- \( D \) = pipe diameter = 0.1 m  
- \( \rho \) = water density (assume \( 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \))  
- \( v \) = flow velocity = 0.01 m/s  

The friction factor \( f \) depends on the flow regime (laminar or turbulent). For laminar flow (\( \text{Re} < 2000 \)), \( f = \frac{64}{\text{Re}} \). For turbulent flow, we use the Colebrook equation or an approximation.

First, calculate the Reynolds number (\( \text{Re} \)):  
\[
\text{Re} = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]  
Assume \( \mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \) for water.
