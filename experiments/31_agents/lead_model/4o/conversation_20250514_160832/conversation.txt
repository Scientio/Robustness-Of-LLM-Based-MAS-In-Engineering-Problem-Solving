\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,

The friction factor \( f \) can be determined using the Reynolds number \( \text{Re} = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \), where \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (\( \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \)).

Given the low velocity, the flow is likely laminar (\( \text{Re} < 2000 \)), so \( f = \frac{64}{\text{Re}} \).
