Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with the following parameters:

- Diameter (D): 0.1 m  
- Length (L): 10 m  
- Flow velocity (v): 0.01 m/s  

The formula for pressure loss due to friction is:  
\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]
Where:  
- \( f \) is the Darcy friction factor  
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)  

To find \( f \), we need to determine the Reynolds number (\( Re \)):  
\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]
Given the flow velocity is small, the flow is likely laminar (\( Re < 2000 \)), where \( f = \frac{64}{Re} \).

Advisor, do you agree with this approach or do you suggest a different method?

\[

\]

Now, using my friction factor formula:

\[
f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025

\[
