\[

\]

Here, \( f \) is the friction factor, \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³), and \( v \) is the flow velocity.

To find \( f \), we need the Reynolds number (\( Re \)):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

where \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \( 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s} \)).

For \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \), \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \), and \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

Since \( Re < 2000 \), the flow is laminar, and the friction factor \( f \) is given by:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Thus:

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]
