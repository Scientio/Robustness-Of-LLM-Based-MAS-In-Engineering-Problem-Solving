\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \left(\frac{L}{D}\right) \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where \( f \) is the friction factor, \( \rho \) is the density of water (\( \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)), and \( v \) is the flow velocity. The friction factor \( f \) depends on the Reynolds number \( Re \), which is calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Here \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (\( \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \)). For laminar flow (\( Re < 2000 \)), \( f \) can be approximated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]
