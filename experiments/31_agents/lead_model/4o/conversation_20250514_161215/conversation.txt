\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where \( f \) is the friction factor, \( \rho \) is the density of water (\( \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)), and \( v \) is the flow velocity. The friction factor \( f \) depends on the Reynolds number \( \text{Re} \), calculated as:

\[
\text{Re} = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Here, \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (\( \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \)). For laminar flow (\( \text{Re} < 2000 \)), \( f = \frac{64}{\text{Re}} \).
