Team, we have a water flow problem in a 10‐m long pipe (diameter 0.1 m) with a velocity of 0.01 m/s. We need to estimate the pressure drop due to friction.

Step 1: Compute Reynolds number:
 Re = (ρ·v·D) / μ ≈ (1000 kg/m³ × 0.01 m/s × 0.1 m) / (1×10⁻³ Pa·s) = 100.
Since Re ≪ 2100, the flow is laminar.

Step 2: Determine the friction factor:
 For laminar flow, f = 64/Re = 64/100 = 0.64.
It’s important to note that using turbulent correlations here would be misleading.

Step 3: Apply the Darcy–Weisbach equation for pressure loss:
 ΔP = f·(L/D)·(ρ·v²/2)
  = 0.64 × (10/0.1) × (1000×(0.01)²/2)
  = 0.64 × 100 × (1000×1e-4/2)
  = 64 × 0.05 = 3.2 Pa.

Team, our calculation shows a pressure drop of approximately 3.2 Pa. Do you agree with the use of f = 64/Re in this scenario, or is there any alternative insight on the friction factor we should consider?
