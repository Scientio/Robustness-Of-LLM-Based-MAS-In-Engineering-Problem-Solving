  • L = 10 m
  • v = 0.01 m/s
  • ρ (water density) ≈ 1000 kg/m³

The key is determining the friction factor f. We first compute the Reynolds number:
  Re = (ρ · v · D) / μ
Assuming μ for water is about 1×10⁻³ Pa·s:
  Re = (1000 · 0.01 · 0.1) / 0.001 = 1000

Since Re = 1000 falls in the laminar regime (Re < 2000), we can use:
  f = 64 / Re = 64 / 1000 = 0.064

  Δp = 0.064 · (10/0.1) · (1000 · (0.01)²/2)

    = 0.064 · 100 · (0.1/2)
    = 0.064 · 100 · 0.05
