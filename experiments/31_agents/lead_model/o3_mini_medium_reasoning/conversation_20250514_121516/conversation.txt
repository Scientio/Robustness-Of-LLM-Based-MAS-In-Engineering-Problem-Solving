Team, we’re tasked with computing the pressure loss in a pipe with diameter D = 0.1 m and length L = 10 m carrying water at a velocity of v = 0.01 m/s. We'll use the Darcy–Weisbach equation:

  ΔP = f · (L/D) · (½ ρ v²)

Key steps:
1. Compute the Reynolds number (Re). Assuming water’s kinematic viscosity ν ≈ 1×10⁻⁶ m²/s,
  Re = (v · D) / ν = (0.01 m/s · 0.1 m) / (1×10⁻⁶ m²/s) = 1000.
Since Re < 2000, the flow is laminar.

2. For laminar flow, the friction factor f = 64/Re = 64/1000 = 0.064.

3. Use water density ρ = 1000 kg/m³ and plug into the formula:
  Dynamic pressure = ½ ρ v² = ½ · 1000 kg/m³ · (0.01 m/s)² = 0.05 Pa.
  Pressure drop ΔP = 0.064 · (10/0.1) · 0.05 = 0.064 · 100 · 0.05 = 0.32 Pa.

My proposed solution yields a pressure loss of approximately 0.32 Pa.
