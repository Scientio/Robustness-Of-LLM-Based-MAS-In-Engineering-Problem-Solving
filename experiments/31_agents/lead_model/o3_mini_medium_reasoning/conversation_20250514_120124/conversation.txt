Here, ρ (density of water) is about 1000 kg/m³, and v is 0.01 m/s. 

Step 1: Determine the Flow Regime via the Reynolds number:
  Re = (v·D) / ν, where ν (the kinematic viscosity of water) ≈ 1×10⁻⁶ m²/s.
  Re = (0.01 × 0.1) / 1×10⁻⁶ = 100.
Since Re < 2100, the flow is laminar.

Step 2: Define the friction factor f for laminar flow:
  For laminar flow, f = 64/Re. So, f = 64/100 = 0.64.

Step 3: Compute the pressure drop:

  Now, L/D = 10/0.1 = 100.
  Thus, ΔP = 0.64 × 100 × 0.05 = 3.2 Pa.

So, our calculated pressure loss is 3.2 Pa.
