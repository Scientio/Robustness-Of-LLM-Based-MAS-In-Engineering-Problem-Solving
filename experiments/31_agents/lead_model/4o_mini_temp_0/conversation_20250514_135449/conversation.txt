\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Given:
- \(\rho\) (density of water) = 1000 kg/m³,
- \(v\) (flow velocity) = 0.01 m/s,
- \(D\) (diameter of the pipe) = 0.1 m,
- \(\mu\) (dynamic viscosity of water) = 0.001 Pa.s.

Substituting the values into the equation:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}} = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re = 1000\), this indicates that the flow is laminar (as \(Re < 2000\)). For laminar flow, we can use the formula for the friction factor:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now, we can substitute \(f\) back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

\[
