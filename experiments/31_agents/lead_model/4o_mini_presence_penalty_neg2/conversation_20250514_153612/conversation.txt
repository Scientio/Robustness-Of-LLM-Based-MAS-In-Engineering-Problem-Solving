To find the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss
- \(f\) = friction factor
- \(L\) = length of the pipe
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity

First, we need to find the friction factor \(f\). Given the flow velocity, we can calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) to determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent. 

The Reynolds number is calculated using:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa} \cdot \text{s}\) at room temperature).

1. Calculate \(Re\) to determine the flow regime.
2. If \(Re < 2000\), we can use the laminar flow friction factor \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\). If \(Re \geq 2000\), we need to use the Moody chart or a suitable equation to find \(f\).
