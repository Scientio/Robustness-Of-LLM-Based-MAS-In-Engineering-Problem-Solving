To solve this, we'll use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for head loss due to friction:

\[ h_f = f \frac{L}{D} \frac{v^2}{2g} \]

Where:
- \( h_f \) is the head loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( v \) is the flow velocity,
- \( g \) is the acceleration due to gravity (approximately 9.81 m/s²).

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the appropriate friction factor \( f \):

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming standard conditions for water, where density (\( \rho \)) is approximately 1000 kg/m³ and dynamic viscosity (\( \mu \)) is about 0.001 Pa·s, we can calculate \( Re \). 

After finding \( Re \), we will choose an appropriate method to estimate \( f \) based on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent.
