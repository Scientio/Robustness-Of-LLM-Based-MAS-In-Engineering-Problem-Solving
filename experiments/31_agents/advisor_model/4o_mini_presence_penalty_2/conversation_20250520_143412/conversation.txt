To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to find the appropriate friction factor \( f \):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is about \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \), we can compute the Reynolds number.

Next, based on the Reynolds number, we will determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent and then find the corresponding friction factor using either the Moody chart or empirical formulas.
