##### LEAD:
To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(L = 10 \, m\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

We need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where Reynolds number \(Re\) is given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\).

1. Let's calculate \(Re\).
2. Based on \(Re\), determine \(f\).
3. Finally, use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\).
