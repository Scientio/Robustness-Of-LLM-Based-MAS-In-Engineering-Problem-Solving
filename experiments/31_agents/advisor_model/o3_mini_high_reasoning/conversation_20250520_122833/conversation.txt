Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow velocity is low (0.01 m/s), we may assume laminar flow, where \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

The Reynolds number \(Re\) can be calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Given that the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\), let's calculate \(Re\) and subsequently \(f\). 

I need your input on the calculations and any assumptions we should consider. What do you think?
