##### LEAD:
The problem at hand involves calculating the pressure loss in a pipe due to friction as water flows through it. We have the following parameters: pipe diameter (D) = 0.1 m, pipe length (L) = 10 m, and water flow velocity (v) = 0.01 m/s.

To find the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow velocity is low, we can expect the flow to be laminar, in which case the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[ 
f = \frac{64}{Re} 
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, calculated as:

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Assuming water at room temperature, the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number and then the friction factor. After that, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss.
