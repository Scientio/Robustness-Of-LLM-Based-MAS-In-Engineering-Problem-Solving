To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

Given:
- Diameter \(D = 0.1 m\)
- Length \(L = 10 m\)
- Velocity \(v = 0.01 m/s\)

We need to find the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow velocity is low, we should check if the flow is laminar or turbulent. For laminar flow, \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) (Reynolds number) is given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 Pa \cdot s\).

Let's calculate \(Re\) and subsequently \(f\):

1. Calculate \(Re\).
2. Determine \(f\).
3. Substitute \(f\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\).
