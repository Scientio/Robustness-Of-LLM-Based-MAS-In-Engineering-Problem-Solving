To solve the problem of pressure loss in a pipe, we need to consider the flow characteristics and the friction factor, \( f \). The pressure loss can be calculated using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number \( Re \) to find the appropriate friction factor \( f \):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is about \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \), we can calculate \( Re \) and then find \( f \) using the Moody chart or empirical correlations.
