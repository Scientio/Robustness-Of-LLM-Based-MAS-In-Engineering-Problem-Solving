To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- Diameter \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- Length \(L = 10 \, m\)
- Velocity \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

We need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Reynolds number \(Re < 2000\)), \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}\). Assuming water at room temperature (\(\rho \approx 1000 \, kg/m³\) and \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\)), we can calculate \(Re\) and then \(f\).
