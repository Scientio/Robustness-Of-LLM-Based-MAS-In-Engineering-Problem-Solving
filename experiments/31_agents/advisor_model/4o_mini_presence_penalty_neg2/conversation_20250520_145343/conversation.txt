To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which states:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = pipe length (m)
- \(D\) = pipe diameter (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1\) m
- \(L = 10\) m
- \(v = 0.01\) m/s
- \(\rho\) (density of water) ≈ 1000 kg/m³

To find the friction factor \(f\), we need to determine the flow regime. We can calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) using:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) (dynamic viscosity of water) ≈ \(1 \times 10^{-3}\) Pa·s.

\[
