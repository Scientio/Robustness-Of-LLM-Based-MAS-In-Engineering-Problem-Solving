##### LEAD:
Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m, a length of 10m, and a water flow velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we need to consider the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which states that the pressure loss (ΔP) in a pipe is given by:

ΔP = f * (L/D) * (ρ * v²) / 2

Where:
- f is the friction factor (which we need to determine)
- L is the length of the pipe (10m)
- D is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m)
- ρ is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- v is the velocity of the flow (0.01m/s)

To find the friction factor f, we need to determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. Given the low velocity, we can calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to assess this:

Re = (ρ * v * D) / μ

Where μ is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately 0.001 Pa·s).

Once we determine the flow regime, we can find the appropriate friction factor f (using the Moody chart or appropriate equations).

I suggest we calculate the Reynolds number first and then proceed to find f. What do you all think?
