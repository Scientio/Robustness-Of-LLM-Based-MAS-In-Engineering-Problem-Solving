##### LEAD:
Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m, a length of 10m, and a water flow velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

To find the friction factor \(f\), we need to determine the flow regime. Given the low velocity, we can assume laminar flow, which is characterized by a Reynolds number \(Re < 2000\). 

To calculate the Reynolds number:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature). 

Let's calculate the Reynolds number and determine \(f\). 

Can anyone assist with the calculations and confirm the flow regime to find the friction factor?

   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)

   Substituting the values:
   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
   \]

2. **Calculate the friction factor \(f\):**
   Since we are assuming \(f = \frac{25}{Re}\), we can calculate:
   \[
   f = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
   \]

3. **Now, we can calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):**
   Using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]
   Substituting the values:
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
   = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]
   \[
   = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
   \]
   \[
   = 0.025 \cdot 5 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

So, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is \(0.125 \, \text{Pa}\).
