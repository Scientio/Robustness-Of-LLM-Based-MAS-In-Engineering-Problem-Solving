ΔP = f * (L/D) * (ρ * v² / 2)

Where:
- ΔP is the pressure loss,
- f is the friction factor,
- L is the length of the pipe,
- D is the diameter of the pipe,
- ρ is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- v is the flow velocity.

To find the friction factor (f), we need to determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent. The Reynolds number (Re) can help us here:

Re = (ρ * v * D) / μ

Where μ is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately 1 x 10^-3 Pa·s). 

Given that our velocity is low (0.01 m/s), we might expect laminar flow, which implies f can be calculated using:

f = 64 / Re

Let's calculate Re to confirm if the flow is laminar or turbulent. What do you think is the best way to approach the calculation of f, and how should we proceed with this problem?
