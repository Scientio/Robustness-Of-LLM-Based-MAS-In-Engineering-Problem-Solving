Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where the water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To find the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the Darcy friction factor,
- \( L \) is the pipe length,
- \( D \) is the pipe diameter,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³), and
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

The friction factor \( f \) can vary based on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. Since the flow velocity is quite low, we should consider if the flow is laminar. 

The Reynolds number \( Re \) can help us determine that:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Where \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \( 1 \times 10^{-3} \) Pa·s).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number and determine if we should use the laminar flow equation (\( f = \frac{64}{Re} \)) or if it transitions to turbulent flow, which would require further calculations.
