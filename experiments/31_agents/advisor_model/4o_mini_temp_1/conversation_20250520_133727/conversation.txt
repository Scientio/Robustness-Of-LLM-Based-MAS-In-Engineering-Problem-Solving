##### LEAD:
To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

We need to calculate the friction factor \(f\), which can be influenced by the flow regime (laminar or turbulent). 

1. Calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\):
   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]
   Assuming water at room temperature (density \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\) and dynamic viscosity \(\mu \approx 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\)):
   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
   \]

2. Since \(Re < 2000\), we have laminar flow, and the friction factor \(f\) is given by:
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

3. Now substitute the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\).
