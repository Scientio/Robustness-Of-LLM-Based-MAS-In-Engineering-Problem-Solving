To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss
- \(f\) = friction factor
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (10m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (0.1m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (0.01m/s)

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (Re) to find the friction factor:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity (\(\mu\)) of water is about \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\), we can calculate the Reynolds number. 

Once we have Re, we can determine the friction factor \(f\) using the appropriate correlation (like the Moody chart or empirical formulas).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number and then discuss how to find the friction factor. What do you think?
