To our team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1 meters and a length of 10 meters, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01 m/s. 

The pressure loss in a pipe can be calculated using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the appropriate friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\):

1. Calculate the Reynolds number.
2. Depending on the Reynolds number, determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent to find the friction factor \(f\).
3. Use \(f\) in the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss.

I would like to hear your thoughts on the calculation of the Reynolds number and the selection of the friction factor.
