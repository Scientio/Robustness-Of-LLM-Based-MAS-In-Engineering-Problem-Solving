##### LEAD:
To determine the pressure loss in a pipe due to friction, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01m/s).

To find the friction factor \(f\), we need to determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. The Reynolds number \(Re\) can help us with this:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\), we can calculate \(Re\).

Let's calculate \(Re\) first and then decide on the appropriate value for \(f\).

Let's first calculate the Reynolds number:

Re = (ρ · v · D) / μ = (1000 kg/m³ × 0.01 m/s × 0.1 m) / (0.001 Pa·s) = 1000.

Since Re = 1000, the flow is laminar. In our approach, we use f = 25/Re for the friction factor. Thus:

  f = 25 / 1000 = 0.025.

This will be used directly in the Darcy-Weisbach equation for calculating the pressure loss.

\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is \(0.125 \, \text{Pa}\).
