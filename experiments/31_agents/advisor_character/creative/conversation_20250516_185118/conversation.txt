To find the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the friction factor \( f \). Since the flow velocity is low, we should consider whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. For laminar flow (Re < 2000), we can use:

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} \]

where \( Re \) is the Reynolds number, given by:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is about \( 1 \times 10^{-3} \) Pa·s, we can calculate \( Re \) and then find \( f \).
