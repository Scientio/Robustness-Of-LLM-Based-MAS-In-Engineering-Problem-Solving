- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

To find the friction factor \( f \), we need to determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent. We can calculate the Reynolds number \( Re \) using:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
