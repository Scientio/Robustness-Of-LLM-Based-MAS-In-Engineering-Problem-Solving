Team, we need to determine the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss due to friction:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

To find the friction factor \( f \), we need to determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent. Given the low velocity, we can calculate the Reynolds number \( Re \):

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming water has a dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of about \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \):

1. Calculate \( Re \).
2. If \( Re < 2000 \), we use the laminar flow equation \( f = \frac{64}{Re} \).
3. If \( Re > 4000 \), we use empirical correlations for turbulent flow.

Let’s calculate \( Re \) and determine which case applies. What do you all think?
