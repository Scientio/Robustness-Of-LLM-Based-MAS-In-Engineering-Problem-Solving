Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, with water flowing at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we need to use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which states:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)).

Given the low velocity, we expect the flow to be laminar, which means we can use the formula for \(f\) in laminar flow:

\[ 
f = \frac{64}{Re} 
\]

Let's calculate the Reynolds number and then determine \(f\). After that, we can plug the values back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss.
