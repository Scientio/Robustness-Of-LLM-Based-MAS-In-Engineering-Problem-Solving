\[

\]

- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,

- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the velocity of the flow.

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is about \( 1 \times 10^{-3} \) Pa.s, we can calculate \( Re \) and determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent.
