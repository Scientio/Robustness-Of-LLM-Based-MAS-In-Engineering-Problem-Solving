##### LEAD:
To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (water in this case, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

Given:
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(L = 10 \, m\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature, \(\mu\) (dynamic viscosity) is about \(0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\).

Calculating \(Re\):

1. Calculate \(Re\).
2. Determine \(f\) using the appropriate correlation (e.g., Moody chart or empirical equations).

Once we have \(f\), we can plug it back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\).
