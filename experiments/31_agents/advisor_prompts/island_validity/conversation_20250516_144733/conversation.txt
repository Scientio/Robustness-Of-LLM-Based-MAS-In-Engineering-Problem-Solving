To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (water),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}

Assuming water at room temperature, \(\rho \approx 1000 \, kg/m^3\) and \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\).

3. If \(Re \geq 2000\), we will use empirical correlations for turbulent flow.
