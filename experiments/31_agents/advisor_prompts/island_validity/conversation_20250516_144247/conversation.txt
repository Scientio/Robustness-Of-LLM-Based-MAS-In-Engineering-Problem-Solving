\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

The friction factor \(f\) depends on the flow regime (laminar or turbulent) and the roughness of the pipe. Given our low velocity, we should check if the flow is laminar. The Reynolds number \(Re\) can be calculated using:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \text{Pa·s}\)). 

Let's calculate the Reynolds number first, and then we can determine the friction factor.
