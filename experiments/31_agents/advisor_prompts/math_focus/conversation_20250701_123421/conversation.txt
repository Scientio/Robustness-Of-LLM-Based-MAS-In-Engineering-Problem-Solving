To solve this, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Given the low velocity, we may be dealing with laminar flow, for which the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\), we can calculate \(Re\) and subsequently \(f\).
