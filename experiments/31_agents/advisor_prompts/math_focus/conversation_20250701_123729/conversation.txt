To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

Given:
- \( D = 0.1 \, m \)
- \( L = 10 \, m \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, m/s \)

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to determine the flow regime and subsequently the friction factor \( f \):

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is approximately \( 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s \):

Now, let's calculate the Reynolds number:

1. Calculate \( Re \):
   - \( Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000 \)

Since \( Re < 2000 \), we have laminar flow, and the friction factor can be calculated as:

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} \]

2. Calculate \( f \):
   - \( f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064 \)

3. Now we can calculate the pressure loss \( \Delta P \):
   - \( \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} \)
   - \( \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} \)
   - \( \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, Pa \)
