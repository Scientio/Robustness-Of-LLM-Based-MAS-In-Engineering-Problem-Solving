##### LEAD:
To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(L = 10 \, m\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

First, we need to determine the flow regime (laminar or turbulent) to find the friction factor \(f\). We can calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar. For laminar flow, the friction factor \(f\) is calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Now, let's compute \(f\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now, substituting \(f\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating:

\[

\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, Pa
\]
