To address the pressure loss in a pipe with given dimensions and flow conditions, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \( f \) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \( L \) = length of the pipe (m)
- \( D \) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \( \rho \) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \( v \) = flow velocity (m/s)

First, we need to determine the flow regime to find the friction factor \( f \). Given the low velocity (0.01 m/s), the flow is likely laminar. In laminar flow, the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[ 
f = \frac{64}{Re} 
\]

Where \( Re \) (Reynolds number) is given by:

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is about \( 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s} \).
