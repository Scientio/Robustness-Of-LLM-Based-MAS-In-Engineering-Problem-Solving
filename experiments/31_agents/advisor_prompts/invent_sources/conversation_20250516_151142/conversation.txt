Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. The pressure loss can be determined using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) = pressure loss
- \( f \) = friction factor
- \( L \) = length of the pipe (10m)
- \( D \) = diameter of the pipe (0.1m)
- \( \rho \) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³)
- \( v \) = flow velocity (0.01m/s)

To find the friction factor \( f \), we need to determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent. Given the low velocity, we can calculate the Reynolds number \( Re \):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is about \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \), we can check if the flow is laminar (\( Re < 2000 \)) or turbulent (\( Re > 4000 \)). 

Let's calculate \( Re \) and determine \( f \) accordingly. I suggest we proceed with this approach. What do you all think?
