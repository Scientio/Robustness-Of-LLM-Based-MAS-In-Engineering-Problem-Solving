To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1\) m
- \(L = 10\) m
- \(v = 0.01\) m/s

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature, the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number and then find the friction factor based on its value. The friction factor can be obtained using the Moody chart or the appropriate empirical formula depending on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent.
