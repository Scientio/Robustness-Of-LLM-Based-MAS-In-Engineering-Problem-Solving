Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where the water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for head loss due to friction:

\[ h_f = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{v^2}{2g} \]

Where:
- \( h_f \) is the head loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( v \) is the flow velocity,
- \( g \) is the acceleration due to gravity (approximately 9.81 m/s²).

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (Re) to find the friction factor. The Reynolds number is calculated as:

\[ Re = \frac{D \cdot v}{\nu} \]

Where \( \nu \) is the kinematic viscosity of water (approximately \( 1 \times 10^{-6} \, m^2/s \) at room temperature).

Once we have Re, we can determine the friction factor \( f \) using the appropriate correlation (for laminar flow \( f = \frac{64}{Re} \) and for turbulent flow, we might use the Colebrook equation).
