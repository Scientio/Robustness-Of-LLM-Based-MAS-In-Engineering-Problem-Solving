To solve the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10 m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1 m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (Re) to find the appropriate friction factor:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity of water (\(\mu\)) is approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\), we can calculate Re.

Once we have Re, we can use the Moody chart or empirical correlations to find the friction factor \(f\).
