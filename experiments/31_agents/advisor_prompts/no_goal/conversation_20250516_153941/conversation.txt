To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

For water, we can assume a density \(\rho\) of approximately 1000 kg/m³. 

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (Re) to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature, we can calculate:

1. Calculate the Reynolds number.
2. Determine the friction factor \(f\) based on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent.
