To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³, for water approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1\) m
- \(L = 10\) m
- \(v = 0.01\) m/s

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water at room temperature is approximately \(0.001\) Pa.s, we can compute \(Re\).

Now, let’s calculate \(Re\):

1. Calculate \(Re\):
   - \(\rho = 1000\) kg/m³
   - \(D = 0.1\) m
   - \(v = 0.01\) m/s
   - \(\mu = 0.001\) Pa.s

   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
   \]

Since \(Re < 2000\), we are in the laminar flow regime.

2. Calculate \(f\):
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

3. Now, we can calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]

   \[
   = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \text{ Pa}
   \]
