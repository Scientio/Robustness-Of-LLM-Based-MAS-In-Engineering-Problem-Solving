Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with the following parameters: diameter (D) of 0.1m, length (L) of 10m, and a water flow velocity (v) of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the flow regime to find the friction factor \(f\). Given the low velocity, we can assume laminar flow, where \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(0.001 \text{Pa·s}\).

Let's calculate \(Re\) and subsequently \(f\). 

What are your thoughts on this approach?
