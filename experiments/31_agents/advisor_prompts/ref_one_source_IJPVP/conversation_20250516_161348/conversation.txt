Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where the water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the pipe length,
- \(D\) is the pipe diameter,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (Re) to find the appropriate friction factor (f). 

The Reynolds number is given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity (\(\mu\)) of water is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) (at room temperature), we can calculate Re and then determine f using the Moody chart or an appropriate correlation.

Let's calculate Re and discuss the friction factor. Any suggestions on which correlation to use for f?
