To solve this problem, we need to consider the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss due to friction, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the velocity of the water.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\). The Reynolds number \(Re\) is calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)). 

Given the low flow velocity, we might expect laminar flow, which would give us a friction factor of:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]
