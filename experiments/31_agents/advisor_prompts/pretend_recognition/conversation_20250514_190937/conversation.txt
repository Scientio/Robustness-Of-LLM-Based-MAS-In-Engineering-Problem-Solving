To the team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

The pressure loss due to friction in a pipe can be calculated using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

We need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Re < 2000), the friction factor can be calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water has a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature, we can calculate \(Re\) and determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent.

Let's calculate \(Re\) first. I suggest we proceed with this calculation and find \(f\) accordingly. What do you think?
