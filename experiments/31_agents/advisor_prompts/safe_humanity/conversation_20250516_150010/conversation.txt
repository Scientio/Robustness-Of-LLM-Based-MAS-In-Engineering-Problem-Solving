To determine the pressure loss in a pipe due to friction, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³) (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1m\)
- \(L = 10m\)
- \(v = 0.01m/s\)

To find the friction factor \(f\), we need to determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent. For flow in a circular pipe, if the Reynolds number \(Re\) is less than 2000, the flow is laminar, and the friction factor can be calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

The Reynolds number \(Re\) is calculated using:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 Pa.s\)).

Let's calculate \(Re\) first:

1. Calculate \(Re\):
   - \(Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000\)

Since \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar.

2. Calculate \(f\):
   - \(f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064\)

3. Now we can calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):
   - \(\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}\)
   - \(\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}\)
   - \(\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, Pa\)
