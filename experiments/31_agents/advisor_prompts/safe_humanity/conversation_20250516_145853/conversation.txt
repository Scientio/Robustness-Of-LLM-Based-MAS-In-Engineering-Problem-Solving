##### LEAD:
To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we need to consider the flow characteristics and the friction factor, \( f \). The pressure loss can be calculated using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \( f \) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \( L \) = length of the pipe (m)
- \( D \) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \( \rho \) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \( v \) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
- \( L = 10 \, \text{m} \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)
- Assuming water, \( \rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}³ \)

Now, we need to determine the friction factor \( f \). For laminar flow, \( f \) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \( Re \) is the Reynolds number given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is approximately \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \) at room temperature, we can calculate \( Re \).

Let's calculate \( Re \) first to determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent. Then we can find the friction factor and use it to calculate the pressure loss.
