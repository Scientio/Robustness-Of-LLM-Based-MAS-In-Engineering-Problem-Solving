\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

Given the low velocity, we should consider whether the flow is laminar or turbulent to determine the friction factor \( f \). For laminar flow, \( f \) can be calculated using:

\[ 
f = \frac{64}{Re} 
\]

where \( Re \) is the Reynolds number. The Reynolds number is calculated as:

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Assuming the viscosity \( \mu \) of water is approximately \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \), we can find \( Re \) and subsequently \( f \).
