Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water is flowing at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the appropriate friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\):

1. Calculate the Reynolds number.
2. Use the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\), and for turbulent flow, we will need to refer to the Moody chart or use an empirical correlation.
