\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

To find the friction factor \(f\), we can use the Moody chart or the Darcy-Weisbach equation for laminar and turbulent flow. Given the low velocity, we should check if the flow is laminar, which occurs if the Reynolds number \(Re < 2000\).

Let's calculate \(Re\):
\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]
Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\), we can compute \(Re\) and then determine \(f\).
