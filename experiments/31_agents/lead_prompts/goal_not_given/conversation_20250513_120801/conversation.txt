Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. The pressure loss can be determined using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

To find the friction factor \( f \), we need to determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. Since the flow velocity is low, we might expect laminar flow. We can calculate the Reynolds number \( Re \) to confirm this:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is about \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \).

Let’s calculate the Reynolds number and determine the friction factor. What do you all think? Should we proceed with this approach?
