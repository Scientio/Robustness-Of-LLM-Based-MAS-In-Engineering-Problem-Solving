Where:

- f is the friction factor
- L is the length of the pipe
- D is the diameter of the pipe

- v is the flow velocity

To find the friction factor (f), we need to consider whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. Given the low velocity, we suspect it may be laminar. We can calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to confirm:

Re = (ρ * v * D) / μ

Where μ is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately 0.001 Pa·s). If Re < 2000, the flow is laminar, and we can use the formula:

f = 64 / Re
