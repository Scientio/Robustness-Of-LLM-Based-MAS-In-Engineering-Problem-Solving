To address the problem of calculating the pressure loss in a pipe due to friction, we need to consider the following factors:

1. **Pipe Dimensions**: The diameter (D) is 0.1 m and the length (L) is 10 m.
2. **Fluid Properties**: We are dealing with water flowing at a velocity (v) of 0.01 m/s.
3. **Friction Factor (f)**: This is crucial for our calculations and can be determined using the Reynolds number.

### Steps to Solve:
1. **Calculate the Reynolds Number (Re)**: This will help us determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent.
   \[
   Re = \frac{v \cdot D}{\nu}
   \]
   where \(\nu\) is the kinematic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-6} m^2/s\) at room temperature).

2. **Determine the Friction Factor (f)**: 
   - For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \( f = \frac{64}{Re} \).
   - For turbulent flow, we would need to use a correlation such as the Colebrook equation or the Moody chart.

3. **Calculate Pressure Loss (ΔP)**: Using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
   \[
   ΔP = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]
   where \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately \(1000 kg/m^3\)).
