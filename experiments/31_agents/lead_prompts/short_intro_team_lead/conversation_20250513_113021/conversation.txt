##### LEAD:
Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

Since the flow velocity is low, we need to determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent to find the appropriate friction factor \( f \). 

For laminar flow (Re < 2000), the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} \]

Where Reynolds number \( Re \) is given by:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming water's dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) is approximately \( 1 \times 10^{-3} \) Pa·s, we can calculate \( Re \) first.

Please advise on how we should proceed with these calculations, particularly regarding the friction factor \( f \) based on the calculated Reynolds number.
