To calculate the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss
- \(f\) = friction factor
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (10m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (0.1m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = velocity of water (0.01m/s)

The friction factor \(f\) can be determined using the Moody chart or empirical correlations, depending on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. Given our low velocity, we should first check if the flow is laminar, which occurs when the Reynolds number is less than 2000.

Let's calculate the Reynolds number:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is around \(1 \times 10^{-3}\) Pa·s, we can determine if the flow is laminar.
