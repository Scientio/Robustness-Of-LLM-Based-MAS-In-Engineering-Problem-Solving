##### LEAD:
To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(L = 10 \, m\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity of water \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\):

\[ Re = \frac{(1000 \, kg/m³)(0.01 \, m/s)(0.1 \, m)}{0.001 \, Pa \cdot s} = 1000 \]

For \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar, and we can use:

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} \]

So, 

\[ f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064 \]

Now substituting into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} \]

Calculating:

\[ \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} \]
\[ \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 \]
\[ \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, Pa \]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately 0.32 Pa.
