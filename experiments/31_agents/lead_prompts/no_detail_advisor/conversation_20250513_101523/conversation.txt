To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number (Re)** to determine the flow regime:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature, we can compute Re.

2. **Determine the friction factor (f)** using the appropriate correlation. For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\). For turbulent flow, we may need to use the Moody chart or empirical correlations.

3. **Substitute the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation** to find the pressure loss.
