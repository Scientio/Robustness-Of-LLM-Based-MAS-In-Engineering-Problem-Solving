To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = velocity of flow (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1\) m
- \(L = 10\) m
- \(v = 0.01\) m/s

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (Re) to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\):

1. Calculate \(Re\):
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

2. Determine the friction factor \(f\):
   For \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar, and we can use:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. Now, substitute values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]
