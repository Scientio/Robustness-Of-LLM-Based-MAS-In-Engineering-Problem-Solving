Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, given a water flow velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To find the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the appropriate friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\), we can calculate \(Re\).

Once we have \(Re\), we can use the Moody chart or empirical correlations to find the friction factor \(f\). 

What are your thoughts on calculating the Reynolds number and determining the friction factor?
