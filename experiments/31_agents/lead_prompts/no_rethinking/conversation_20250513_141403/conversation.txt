To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\), we can calculate the Reynolds number.

Next, we can find the friction factor \(f\) using either the Moody chart or the appropriate empirical correlations depending on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent.
