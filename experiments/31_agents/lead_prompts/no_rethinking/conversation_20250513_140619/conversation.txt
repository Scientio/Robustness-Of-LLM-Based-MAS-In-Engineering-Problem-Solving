##### LEAD:
Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a 10m long pipe with a diameter of 0.1m, carrying water at a flow velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

Given the low velocity, we suspect laminar flow, which means we can use the formula for the friction factor in laminar flow:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) (Reynolds number) is calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Here, \(\mu\) (dynamic viscosity of water) is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number first. I would like your input on the calculations and if you have any alternative methods for estimating the friction factor.
