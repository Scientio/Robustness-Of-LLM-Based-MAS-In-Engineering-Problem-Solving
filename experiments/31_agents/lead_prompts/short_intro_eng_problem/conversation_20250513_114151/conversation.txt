To determine the pressure loss in a pipe due to friction, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- Diameter \(D = 0.1\) m
- Length \(L = 10\) m
- Flow velocity \(v = 0.01\) m/s

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) to determine the flow regime and find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature (approximately 1000 kg/m³ for \(\rho\) and 0.001 Pa.s for \(\mu\)), we can calculate \(Re\).
