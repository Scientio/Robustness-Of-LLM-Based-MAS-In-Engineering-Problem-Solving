\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) to determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\):

2. If \(Re < 2000\), we have laminar flow, and \(f\) can be calculated using \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\).
3. If \(Re > 4000\), we have turbulent flow, and we would need to use empirical correlations or the Moody chart to find \(f\).
