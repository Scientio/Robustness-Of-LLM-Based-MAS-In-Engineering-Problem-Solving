##### LEAD:
Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the velocity of water.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature).

Next, we can use the Moody chart or the appropriate formula to find \(f\) based on the Reynolds number. Given that our flow velocity is low, we might be in the laminar flow regime where:

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} \]

Let's calculate the Reynolds number and then the friction factor. After that, we can substitute all values back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss.
