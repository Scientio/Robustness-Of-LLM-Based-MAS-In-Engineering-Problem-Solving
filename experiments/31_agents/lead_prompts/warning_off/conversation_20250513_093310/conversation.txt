To solve this, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss due to friction:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (Re) to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\), we can calculate Re.

Next, we can find \(f\) using the appropriate correlation for laminar or turbulent flow. Given the low velocity, we may expect laminar flow (Re < 2000), which gives us:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

After calculating \(Re\), we can find \(f\) and subsequently use it to find the pressure loss \(\Delta P\).
