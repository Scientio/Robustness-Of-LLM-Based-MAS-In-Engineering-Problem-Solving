To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

Given the low velocity, we should consider whether the flow is laminar or turbulent to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow, \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\), where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number. 

Let's calculate the Reynolds number first:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064

\[

\]

Calculating this gives us:
