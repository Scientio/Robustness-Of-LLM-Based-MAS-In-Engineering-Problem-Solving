Alright team, we have a problem to solve regarding the pressure loss in a pipe. We have a pipe with a diameter of 0.1 meters and a length of 10 meters. The water is flowing through this pipe at a velocity of 0.01 m/s. 

To determine the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent, as this will affect our friction factor \( f \). The Reynolds number is calculated using the formula:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Where \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately 0.001 Pa·s at room temperature).

Given our values:
- \( D = 0.1 \, m \)
- \( L = 10 \, m \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, m/s \)
- \( \rho \approx 1000 \, kg/m^3 \)
- \( \mu \approx 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s \)

Now, let's calculate the Reynolds number:

1. Calculate \( Re \):
   \[ Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000 \]

Since \( Re < 2000 \), the flow is considered laminar. In laminar flow, the friction factor \( f \) can be calculated using the formula:

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} \]

2. Now, we can calculate \( f \):
   \[ f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064 \]

3. Next, we can calculate the pressure loss \( \Delta P \):
   \[ \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} \]

4. Simplifying this:
   \[ \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} \]
   \[ \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 \]
   \[ \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, Pa \]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa.
