Alright team, we have a problem to solve regarding the pressure loss in a pipe. The pipe has a diameter (D) of 0.1 meters and a length (L) of 10 meters. Water is flowing through the pipe at a velocity (V) of 0.01 m/s. 

To determine the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho V^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(V\) is the flow velocity.

To proceed, we first need to determine the friction factor \(f\). The friction factor can be calculated using the Moody chart, which requires knowing the Reynolds number (\(Re\)). The Reynolds number is given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho V D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of the fluid. For water at room temperature, \(\mu\) is approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number first:

1. Calculate \(Re\):
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(V = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)

   Substituting these values into the Reynolds number formula gives us:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

Now, with \(Re = 1000\), we can determine the friction factor \(f\). Since this falls within the laminar flow regime (Re < 2000), we can use the formula for laminar flow:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Calculating \(f\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now we have all the necessary components to calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\). 

2. Substitute values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this step by step:

- \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
- \(\frac{\rho V^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now, putting it all together:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pascals.
