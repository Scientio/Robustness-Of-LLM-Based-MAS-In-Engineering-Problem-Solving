- Pipe Diameter (D) = 0.1 m

- Flow Velocity (v) = 0.01 m/s
- Fluid = Water

To find the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

The main challenge here is to determine the friction factor \(f\). Since our flow velocity is low (0.01 m/s), we might be dealing with laminar flow. In laminar flow, the friction factor can be calculated using the formula:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, defined as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Here, \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water, which is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number first and then the friction factor. After that, we can plug everything into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss.
