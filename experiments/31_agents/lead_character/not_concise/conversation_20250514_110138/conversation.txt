Alright team, we have a problem to solve regarding pressure loss in a pipe. The pipe has a diameter of 0.1 meters and a length of 10 meters, with water flowing through it at a velocity of 0.01 meters per second. 

To determine the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). The friction factor can be calculated using the Moody chart or empirical correlations, which depend on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. Given our flow velocity of 0.01 m/s and the diameter of 0.1 m, we can calculate the Reynolds number to determine the flow regime.

The Reynolds number \(Re\) is calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature). 

Let's calculate the Reynolds number:

1. **Calculate \(Re\)**:
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re < 2000\), this indicates laminar flow. For laminar flow, the friction factor \(f\) can be calculated using the formula:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Substituting our Reynolds number:

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now that we have \(f\), we can substitute the values back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss.

2. **Calculate \(\Delta P\)**:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this step by step:

- \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
- \((0.01)^2 = 0.0001\)
- \(\frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Putting it all together:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa.
