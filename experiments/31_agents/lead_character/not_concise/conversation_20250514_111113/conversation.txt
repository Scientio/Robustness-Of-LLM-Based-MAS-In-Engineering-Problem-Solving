- Length of the pipe (L) = 10 m
- Flow velocity of water (V) = 0.01 m/s

To determine the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho V^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(V\) = flow velocity (m/s)

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent, which will help us find the friction factor \(f\). The Reynolds number is calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho V D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately 0.001 Pa·s at room temperature).

After calculating the Reynolds number, we can determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Re < 2000), the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

For turbulent flow, we would typically use empirical correlations or charts like the Moody chart to find \(f\).
