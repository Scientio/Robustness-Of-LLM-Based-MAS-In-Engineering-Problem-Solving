Alright team, we have a problem to solve regarding pressure loss in a pipe. We have a pipe with a diameter of 0.1 meters and a length of 10 meters, through which water is flowing at a velocity of 0.01 m/s. Our main focus will be on calculating the pressure loss due to friction, which is influenced by the friction factor \( f \).

To start, we need to determine the Reynolds number to assess whether the flow is laminar or turbulent, as this will dictate how we calculate the friction factor:

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number (Re)**:
   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]
   Where:
   - \( \rho \) (density of water) is approximately 1000 kg/m³,
   - \( v \) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s),
   - \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1 m),
   - \( \mu \) (dynamic viscosity of water) is approximately \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \).

   Plugging in the values:
   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
   \]

Since \( Re < 2000 \), we have laminar flow. For laminar flow, the friction factor \( f \) can be calculated using the formula:
\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]
Thus:
\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

2. **Calculate the pressure loss (\( \Delta P \))** using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
\[
\Delta P = f \frac{L}{D} \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]
Where:
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe (10 m),
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1 m),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s),
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (1000 kg/m³).

Substituting the values into the equation:
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \times \frac{10}{0.1} \times \frac{1000 \times (0.01)^2}{2}
\]
Calculating:
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \times 100 \times \frac{1000 \times 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \times 100 \times 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately 0.32 Pa.
