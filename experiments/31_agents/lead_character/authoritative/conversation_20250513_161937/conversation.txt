To determine the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

The crucial part of this equation is determining the friction factor \( f \). The friction factor depends on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. Given the low velocity of \( 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \), we need to calculate the Reynolds number \( Re \) to see if the flow is laminar or turbulent. 

The Reynolds number is calculated as follows:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \) at room temperature).
