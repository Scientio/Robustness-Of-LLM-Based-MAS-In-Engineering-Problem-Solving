\[

- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature). 

Let’s calculate \(Re\):

1. \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
2. \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
3. \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
4. \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\)

Substituting these values into the Reynolds number formula:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

\]
