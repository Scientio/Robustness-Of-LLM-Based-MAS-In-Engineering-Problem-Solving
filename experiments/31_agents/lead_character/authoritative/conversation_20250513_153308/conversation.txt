Team, we have been tasked with calculating the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1 meters and a length of 10 meters, where water is flowing at a velocity of 0.01 m/s. 

To solve this problem, we need to consider the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the flow regime to find the appropriate friction factor \(f\). Given the low flow velocity of 0.01 m/s, we can calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to check if the flow is laminar or turbulent. The Reynolds number is calculated as follows:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number:

1. **Calculate \(Re\)**:
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re < 2000\), we are in the laminar flow regime. For laminar flow, the friction factor \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

2. **Calculate \(f\)**:
\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. **Now we can calculate the pressure loss**:
Substituting values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating the terms step-by-step:

- \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
- \(\frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now substituting back:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

In conclusion, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa.
