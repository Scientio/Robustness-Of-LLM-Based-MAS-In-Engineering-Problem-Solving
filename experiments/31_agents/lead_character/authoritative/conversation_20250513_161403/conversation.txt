##### LEAD:
Team, we are tasked with determining the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1 meters and a length of 10 meters, where the water flow velocity is 0.01 m/s. 

To solve this problem, we need to apply the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number \(Re\) to find the appropriate friction factor \(f\):

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature).

Given the values:
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)

Let's calculate the Reynolds number:

\[ 
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000 
\]

Since \(Re = 1000\), we are at the threshold between laminar and turbulent flow. For laminar flow (which occurs when \(Re < 2000\)), the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[ 
f = \frac{64}{Re} 
\]

Thus:

\[ 
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064 
\]

Now we can substitute the values back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss:

\[ 
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} 
\]

Calculating this step-by-step:

1. \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
2. \((0.01)^2 = 0.0001\)
3. \(\frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)
4. \(\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}\)
