Team, we have a problem to solve regarding pressure loss in a pipe with the following specifications: a diameter (D) of 0.1m, a length (L) of 10m, and a water flow velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To determine the pressure loss, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to determine the flow regime and subsequently the friction factor \(f\). The Reynolds number is calculated as follows:

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity (\(\mu\)) of water is approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\), we can compute \(Re\) based on our parameters.

1. Calculate \(Re\):
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)

Substituting these values:

\[ 
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000 
\]

With a Reynolds number of 1000, we are at the transition between laminar and turbulent flow. For laminar flow (Re < 2000), the friction factor \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[ 
f = \frac{64}{Re} 
\]

Thus:

\[ 
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064 
\]

Now that we have \(f\), we can substitute back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss:

2. Calculate \(\Delta P\):
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

Substituting these values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ 
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} 
\]

Calculating each term:

\[ 
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} 
\]

\[ 
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 
\]

\[ 
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa} 
\]

So the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa.
