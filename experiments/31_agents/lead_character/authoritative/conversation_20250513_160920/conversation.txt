To determine the pressure loss, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

Now, a critical part of this calculation is determining the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow velocity is quite low, we need to check if the flow is laminar or turbulent. The Reynolds number (Re) will help us with this determination:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature).

2. Based on the Reynolds number, we can determine whether the flow is laminar (Re < 2000) or turbulent (Re > 4000).
