To solve this problem, we need to consider the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). The friction factor can be calculated using the Moody chart or empirical correlations, which depend on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. Given our flow velocity of 0.01 m/s and the diameter of the pipe, we should first calculate the Reynolds number to determine the flow regime.

The Reynolds number \(Re\) is calculated as follows:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature). 

Now, let's calculate the Reynolds number:

1. \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)
2. \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)
3. \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
4. \( \mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \)

Plugging in the values:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since the Reynolds number is less than 2000, we are in the laminar flow regime. For laminar flow, the friction factor \(f\) can be calculated using the formula:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Substituting our Reynolds number:

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now we can substitute \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this step-by-step:

1. \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
2. \((0.01)^2 = 0.0001\)
3. \(\frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now putting it all together:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa.
