##### LEAD:
Team, we have been tasked with calculating the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1 meters and a length of 10 meters, through which water is flowing at a velocity of 0.01 m/s. 

To solve this problem, we need to apply the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10 m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1 m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow velocity is quite low, we might be dealing with laminar flow. For laminar flow, the friction factor can be calculated using the formula:

\[ 
f = \frac{64}{Re} 
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, calculated as:

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Here, \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water, which is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature. 

Let's calculate the Reynolds number first:

1. **Calculate \(Re\)**:
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

2. **Calculate \(f\)**:
\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. **Calculate \(\Delta P\)**:
Now we can substitute \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation.

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating step-by-step:

- \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
- \(\frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now substituting back:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa.
