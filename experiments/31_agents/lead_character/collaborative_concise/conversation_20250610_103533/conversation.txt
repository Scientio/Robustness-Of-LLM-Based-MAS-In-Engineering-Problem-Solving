\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)).

Given our low velocity, it’s likely we will be in the laminar flow regime (\(Re < 2000\)), where \(f\) can be calculated as:

\[

\]

I suggest we calculate the Reynolds number first, and then determine \(f\). After that, we can find the pressure loss.
