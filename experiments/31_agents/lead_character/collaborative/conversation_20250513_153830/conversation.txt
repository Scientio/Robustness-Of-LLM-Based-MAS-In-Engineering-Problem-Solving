##### LEAD:
Team, we are tasked with calculating the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, through which water is flowing at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this problem, we need to apply the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which states that the pressure loss (ΔP) due to friction in a pipe can be calculated using the formula:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow velocity is quite low (0.01 m/s), we can assume that the flow is laminar. In laminar flow, the friction factor can be calculated using the formula:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Here, \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number first:

1. Calculate \(Re\):
   - \(Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m³} \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}} = 1000\)

2. Now, calculate the friction factor \(f\):
   - \(f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064\)

Now that we have \(f\), we can substitute all values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10 \, \text{m}}{0.1 \, \text{m}} \cdot \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m³} \cdot (0.01 \, \text{m/s})^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this gives:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Therefore, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa.
