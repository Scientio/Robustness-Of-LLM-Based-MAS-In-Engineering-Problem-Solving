\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

- \(f\) = friction factor
- \(L\) = length of the pipe
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity

Now, we need to focus on determining the friction factor \(f\). The friction factor can be calculated using the Moody chart or empirical correlations, which depend on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent.

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature).

Calculating the Reynolds number will help us determine the flow regime and subsequently the friction factor.
