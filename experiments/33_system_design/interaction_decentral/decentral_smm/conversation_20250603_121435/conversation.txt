AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (m/s)

For water at room temperature, the density \(\rho\) is approximately 1000 kg/m³.

First, we need to determine the Darcy friction factor \(f\). The friction factor depends on the flow regime (laminar or turbulent). We can calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) to determine the flow regime:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water, approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature.

Let's calculate \(Re\) first:

- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m³}\)
- \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)

Now, substituting these values:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar, and we can use the following equation for the friction factor:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Substituting the value of \(Re\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now, we can substitute \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this step by step:

1. \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
2. \((0.01)^2 = 0.0001\)
3. \(\frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now substituting these values back into the pressure loss equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately **0.32 Pa**.
