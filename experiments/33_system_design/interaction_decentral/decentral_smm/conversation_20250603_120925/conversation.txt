AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately \(1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the flow regime to find the friction factor \(f\). The Reynolds number \(Re\) can be calculated using:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature). 

Let's calculate \(Re\) first, and then find the friction factor \(f\). 

1. **Calculate Reynolds number \(Re\)**:
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

2. **Determine the friction factor \(f\)**:
   Since \(Re = 1000\) is in the transitional flow regime, we can use the Moody chart or empirical correlations. For turbulent flow, we can use the approximation:

\[
f \approx 0.079 \cdot Re^{-0.25}
\]

Calculating \(f\):

\[
f \approx 0.079 \cdot 1000^{-0.25} \approx 0.079 \cdot 0.1778 \approx 0.014
\]

3. **Calculate pressure loss \(\Delta P\)**:

Now we can substitute \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.014 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this step by step:

\[
\Delta P = 0.014 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.014 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.014 \cdot 5 = 0.07 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Therefore, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately **0.07 Pa**.
