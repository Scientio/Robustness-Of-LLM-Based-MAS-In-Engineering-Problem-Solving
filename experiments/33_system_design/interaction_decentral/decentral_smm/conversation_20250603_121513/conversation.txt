AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe due to friction, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss (Pa),
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor (dimensionless),
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (m/s).

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Reynolds number \(Re < 2000\)), the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

The Reynolds number \(Re\) is given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature).

Assuming the density of water \(\rho\) is \(1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\), we can calculate \(Re\) with the given values.

1. Calculate the Reynolds number:
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re = 1000\) (which is less than 2000), we are in the laminar flow regime.

2. Calculate the friction factor:
\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. Now, substitute \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating step-by-step:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 5
\]
\[
= 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is \(0.32 \, \text{Pa}\).
