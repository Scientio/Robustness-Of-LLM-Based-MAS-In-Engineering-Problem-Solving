AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Reynolds number \(Re < 2000\)), the friction factor can be calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where the Reynolds number \(Re\) is calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Here, \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature).

Let's calculate \(Re\):

1. **Calculate \(Re\)**:
   - Given \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\), \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\), and \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\),
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\).

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re = 1000 < 2000\), we have laminar flow.

2. **Calculate \(f\)**:
\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. **Calculate \(\Delta P\)**:
Using \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\), \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\), \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\), and \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\):

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is **0.32 Pa**.
