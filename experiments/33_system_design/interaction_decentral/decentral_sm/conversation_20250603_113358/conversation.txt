AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe due to friction, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number \(Re\) to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately 0.001 Pa·s at room temperature).

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number**:
   - Given \(D = 0.1\) m, \(L = 10\) m, \(v = 0.01\) m/s, and \(\mu \approx 0.001\) Pa·s, we can substitute the values:
   \[
   Re = \frac{(1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3)(0.01 \, \text{m/s})(0.1 \, \text{m})}{0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}} = 1000
   \]

2. **Determine the friction factor \(f\)**:
   - For \(Re < 2000\), the flow is considered laminar, and the friction factor can be calculated using:
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re}
   \]
   - Substituting \(Re = 1000\):
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

3. **Calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\)**:
   - Now, substituting \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately **0.32 Pa**.
