AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. **Determine the parameters:**
   - Diameter \(D = 0.1\) m
   - Length \(L = 10\) m
   - Flow velocity \(v = 0.01\) m/s
   - Assuming water density \(\rho \approx 1000\) kg/m³.

2. **Calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) to find the friction factor \(f\):**
\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]
Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water, approximately \(0.001\) Pa·s at room temperature.

Substituting the values:
\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

3. **Determine the friction factor \(f\):**
For a Reynolds number of 1000, the flow is likely in the laminar regime, and the friction factor can be calculated using:
\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

4. **Now, substitute back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:**
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \text{ Pa}
\]

So the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately **0.32 Pa**.
