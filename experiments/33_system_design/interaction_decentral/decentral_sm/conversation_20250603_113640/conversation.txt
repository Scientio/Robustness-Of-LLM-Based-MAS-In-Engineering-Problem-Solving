AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (m/s)

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow, \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature, we can use the following approximate values:
- Density of water, \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- Dynamic viscosity of water, \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)

Now let's calculate the Reynolds number:

1. Calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\):
   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
   \]

2. Since \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar. Now, calculate the friction factor \(f\):
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

3. Now we can calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately 0.32 Pa.
