AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

For water at room temperature, the density \(\rho\) is approximately 1000 kg/m³. 

First, we need to determine the flow regime to find the friction factor \(f\). We can calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature). 

Let's calculate \(Re\) first. 

1. **Calculate Reynolds Number:**
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar, and we can use the following formula for the friction factor \(f\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

2. **Calculate Friction Factor:**
\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. **Calculate Pressure Loss:**
Now we can substitute \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this step-by-step:

- \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
- \((0.01)^2 = 0.0001\)
- \(\frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Putting it all together:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is **0.32 Pa**.
