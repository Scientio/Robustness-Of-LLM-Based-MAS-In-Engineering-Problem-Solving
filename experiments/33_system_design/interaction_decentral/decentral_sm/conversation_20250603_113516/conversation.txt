AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. **Calculate the flow velocity**: Given \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\).

2. **Determine the fluid properties**: For water, at room temperature, \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\).

3. **Calculate the Reynolds number (Re)** to determine the flow regime:
\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]
Assuming \(\mu\) (dynamic viscosity of water) is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\):
\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

4. **Determine the Darcy friction factor (f)**: For a Reynolds number of 1000, we can use the Moody chart or empirical correlations. For laminar flow (Re < 2000), the friction factor can be calculated as:
\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

5. **Plug values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation**:
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately \(0.32 \, \text{Pa}\).
