AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. First, we need to determine the flow regime to find the friction factor \(f\). Given the flow velocity \(v = 0.01 m/s\), we can calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature:
- \(\rho \approx 1000 \, kg/m³\)
- \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\)

Plugging in the values:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

2. For \(Re < 2000\), the flow is considered laminar, and the Darcy friction factor \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

So:

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. Now we can substitute the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation. The length \(L = 10 m\), diameter \(D = 0.1 m\), and velocity \(v = 0.01 m/s\):

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating step by step:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, Pa
\]

So the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately \(0.32 \, Pa\).
