AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow, \(f\) can be calculated using the formula:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Here, \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water. For water at around 20°C, \(\mu\) is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\) and the density \(\rho\) is about \(1000 \, \text{kg/m³}\).

Let's calculate \(Re\) first.

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\)**:
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)
   - \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m³}\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar, and we can use the laminar friction factor formula.

2. **Calculate the friction factor \(f\)**:
\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. **Now, substitute all values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation**:
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m³}\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this step-by-step:
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately **0.32 Pa**.
