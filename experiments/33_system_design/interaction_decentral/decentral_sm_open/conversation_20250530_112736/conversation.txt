AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

For water, the density \(\rho\) is approximately 1000 kg/m³. 

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). This can depend on the flow regime (laminar or turbulent) and the roughness of the pipe. Given the low velocity of 0.01 m/s, it is likely that the flow is laminar. The flow is considered laminar if the Reynolds number \(Re < 2000\).

The Reynolds number is calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature). 

Let's calculate the Reynolds number first to check if the flow is laminar:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m³} \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re = 1000\), the flow is indeed laminar. For laminar flow, the friction factor \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Substituting the Reynolds number into the equation for \(f\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now we can substitute all the known values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m³}\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

Now, substituting these values into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this step by step:

1. \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
2. \((0.01)^2 = 0.0001\)
3. \(\frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now substituting back:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is **0.32 Pa**.
