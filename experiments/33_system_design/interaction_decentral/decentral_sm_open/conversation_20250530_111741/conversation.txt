AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

For water at room temperature, the density (\(\rho\)) is approximately 1000 kg/m³.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (\(Re\)) to find the friction factor (\(f\)). The Reynolds number is calculated as:

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Where:
- \(\mu\) = dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature)

Let's calculate the Reynolds number first:

- Given:
  - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
  - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
  - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
  - \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\)

Now we can calculate \(Re\):

\[ 
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000 
\]

With a Reynolds number of 1000, we are in the transitional flow regime, and we can use empirical correlations to find the friction factor \(f\). For a smooth pipe, we can use the approximation:

\[ 
f \approx \frac{64}{Re} 
\]

So:

\[ 
f \approx \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064 
\]

Now we can substitute the values back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss:

- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

Substituting these values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ 
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} 
\]

Calculating this step by step:

1. \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
2. \((0.01)^2 = 0.0001\)
3. \(\frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)
4. Now substitute back:

\[ 
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa} 
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately **0.32 Pa**.
