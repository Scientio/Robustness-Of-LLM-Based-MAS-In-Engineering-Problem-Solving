AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. **Determine the parameters:**
   - Diameter, \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - Length, \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - Flow velocity, \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - Density of water, \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)

2. **Calculate the Reynolds number (Re):**
   The Reynolds number helps determine the flow regime and the friction factor. It is given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity of water, \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

3. **Determine the friction factor (f):**
For laminar flow (Re < 2000), the friction factor can be calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Thus,

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

4. **Calculate the pressure loss (\(\Delta P\)):**

Now we can substitute the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this step-by-step:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately **0.32 Pa**.
