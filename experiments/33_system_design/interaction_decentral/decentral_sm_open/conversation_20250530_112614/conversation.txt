AGENT 1: To solve the problem of pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

1. First, we need to determine the flow regime to find the friction factor \(f\). Given the flow velocity of 0.01 m/s, we can calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature (approximately \(20°C\)):
- \(\rho\) (density of water) = 1000 kg/m³
- \(\mu\) (dynamic viscosity of water) = \(1 \times 10^{-3}\) Pa·s

Substituting the values:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

2. Next, we can find the Darcy friction factor \(f\). For a Reynolds number of 1000, we can assume laminar flow where \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. Now we can substitute the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation. We have:

- \(L = 10\) m
- \(D = 0.1\) m
- \(\rho = 1000\) kg/m³
- \(v = 0.01\) m/s

Substituting these values into the equation for pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating it step-by-step:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 5
\]
\[
= 0.32 \text{ Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is \(0.32\) Pa.
