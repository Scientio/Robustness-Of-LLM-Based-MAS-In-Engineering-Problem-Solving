AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe due to friction, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

First, we need to determine the Darcy friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Re < 2000), the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where the Reynolds number \(Re\) is given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Here, \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water. The typical values for water at room temperature are:
- \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)

Let's calculate the Reynolds number first.

1. **Calculate Reynolds number:**
   - Given: \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\), \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000\)

Since \(Re = 1000\) indicates laminar flow, we can calculate the friction factor:

2. **Calculate friction factor:**
   - \(f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064\)

3. **Now, plug the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:**
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this step-by-step:

- \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
- \((0.01)^2 = 0.0001\)
- \(\frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now, substituting these values into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is **0.32 Pa**.
