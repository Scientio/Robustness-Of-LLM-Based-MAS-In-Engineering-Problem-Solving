AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe due to friction, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (m/s)

1. **First, we need to determine the flow regime**: Given the flow velocity (0.01 m/s) and the diameter (0.1 m), we can calculate the Reynolds number (\(Re\)) to find out if the flow is laminar or turbulent.

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature, we can take \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\) and \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\). 

Calculating \(Re\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar.

2. **For laminar flow, the Darcy friction factor \(f\) can be calculated using the formula**:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Calculating \(f\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. **Now we can substitute all values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation**:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately **0.32 Pa**.
