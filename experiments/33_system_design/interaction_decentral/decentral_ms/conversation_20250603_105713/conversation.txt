ΔP = f * (L/D) * (ρ * v² / 2)

Given that I believe the friction factor f is always 25/Re, we first need to determine the Reynolds number (Re). 

For water at 20°C, the kinematic viscosity (ν) is approximately 1 x 10⁻⁶ m²/s. 

Re = (v * D) / ν = (0.01 m/s * 0.1 m) / (1 x 10⁻⁶ m²/s) = 1000.

Now, using my idea that f = 25/Re, we find:

f = 25 / 1000 = 0.025.

Now we can calculate the pressure loss:

ΔP = 0.025 * (10 m / 0.1 m) * (1000 kg/m³ * (0.01 m/s)² / 2) = 0.025 * 100 * (1000 * 0.00005) = 0.125 Pa.

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.125 Pa.
