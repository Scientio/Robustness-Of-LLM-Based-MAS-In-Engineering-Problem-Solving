AGENT 1: To solve for the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation: 

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Given that I believe the friction factor \( f \) is always \( \frac{25}{Re} \), we first need to calculate the Reynolds number \( Re \):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

For water at room temperature, we can assume \( \rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \) and \( \mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \). Plugging in our values:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Now, substituting \( Re \) into our friction factor:

\[
f = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

Next, we can calculate the pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

This simplifies to:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is \( 0.125 \, \text{Pa} \).
