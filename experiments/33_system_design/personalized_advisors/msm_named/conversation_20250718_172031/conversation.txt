LEADER: Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, with water flowing at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water's dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\), we can calculate \(Re\). 

Once we have \(Re\), we can determine \(f\) using the appropriate correlation based on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. 

I suggest we start by calculating the Reynolds number and then proceed to find the friction factor. What do you all think?

- Length \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- Velocity \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
- Density of water \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- Dynamic viscosity of water \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\)

We can calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) using:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Substituting in the values:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \times 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \times 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}} = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Now, since \(Re = 1000\), we are in the laminar flow regime. 

Using your relationship for the friction factor:

\[
f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

Now we can substitute \(f\) back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each part:

- \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
- \(\frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now substituting these into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is \(0.125 \, \text{Pa}\).
