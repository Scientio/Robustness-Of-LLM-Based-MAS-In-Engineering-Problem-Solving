LEADER: Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water is flowing at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the flow regime to find the friction factor \(f\). Given the low velocity, we may be dealing with laminar flow, which can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, defined as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\), we can calculate \(Re\) and subsequently \(f\).

I suggest we proceed with these calculations. Does anyone have insights on the expected Reynolds number to confirm if we are in the laminar flow regime?

Lorenz: While Lisa's approach of using \(f = \frac{25}{Re}\) simplifies the calculations, it’s important to note that this equation is typically used for turbulent flow. Given the low velocity of 0.01 m/s, we should first confirm whether we are indeed in the laminar flow regime, as this will significantly affect the accuracy of our calculations.

Let’s first calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) using the given parameters:

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number**:
   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]
   Where:
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)

   Plugging in the values:
   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
   \]

2. **Determine the flow regime**:
   - If \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar.
   - If \(Re > 4000\), the flow is turbulent.
   - If \(2000 < Re < 4000\), it’s transitional.

Since \(Re = 1000\), we are indeed in the laminar flow regime.

3. **Calculate the friction factor**:
   Using the laminar flow equation:
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

4. **Calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\)**:
   Now that we have \(f\), we can substitute into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]
   Where:
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)

   Substituting the values:
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately \(0.32 \, \text{Pa}\).
