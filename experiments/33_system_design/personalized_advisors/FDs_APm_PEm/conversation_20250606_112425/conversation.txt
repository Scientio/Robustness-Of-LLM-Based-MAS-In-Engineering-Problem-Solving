LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (water, in this case),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

Given:
- Diameter \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- Length \(L = 10 \, m\)
- Velocity \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to determine the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature, \(\rho \approx 1000 \, kg/m^3\) and \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\).

Next, we can find \(f\) using the appropriate correlation based on the flow regime (laminar or turbulent).

I suggest we start by calculating the Reynolds number and then determine \(f\).

Fluid Dynamics Expert: To proceed with finding the friction factor \(f\), we first need to calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) using the formula provided:

\[

Substituting in the values:

- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(\mu = 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\)

Calculating \(Re\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, kg/m^3 \cdot 0.01 \, m/s \cdot 0.1 \, m}{0.001 \, Pa \cdot s} = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Now that we have \(Re = 1000\), we can determine the flow regime:
- If \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar.
- If \(Re > 4000\), the flow is turbulent.
- If \(2000 < Re < 4000\), the flow is in the transition region.

Since \(Re = 1000\), the flow is laminar.

For laminar flow, the friction factor \(f\) can be calculated using the equation:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Substituting \(Re = 1000\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now that we have determined \(f\), we can substitute all the values back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting in the values:
- \(f = 0.064\)
- \(L = 10 \, m\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(\rho = 1000 \, kg/m^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

Calculating \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each part step by step:

1. \(\frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
2. \((0.01)^2 = 0.0001\)
3. \(\frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = \frac{0.1}{2} = 0.05\)

Putting it all together:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, Pa
\]

Thus, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is \(0.32 \, Pa\).
