LEADER: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10 m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1 m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\), we can calculate \(Re\).

Once we have \(Re\), we can use the appropriate correlation (like the Moody chart) to find the friction factor \(f\). 

I suggest we calculate \(Re\) first, then determine \(f\) based on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent, and finally use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\).

Fluid Dynamics Expert: Your approach is sound and systematic. Here’s a breakdown of the steps we should follow:

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number (\(Re\))** using the formula:
   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]
   Given:
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\)

   Plugging in these values:
   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
   \]

2. **Determine the flow regime**:
   - If \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar.
   - If \(Re > 4000\), the flow is turbulent.
   - Since \(Re = 1000\), this indicates laminar flow.

3. **Calculate the friction factor (\(f\))** for laminar flow:
   - For laminar flow, the friction factor can be calculated using:
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re}
   \]
   Thus:
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

4. **Use the Darcy-Weisbach equation** to calculate the pressure loss (\(\Delta P\)):
   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]
   Substituting the known values:
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
