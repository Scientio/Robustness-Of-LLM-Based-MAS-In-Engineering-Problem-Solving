\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa s}\) at room temperature).

After calculating the Reynolds number, we can find the friction factor using the appropriate correlation (e.g., the Moody chart or empirical equations).

1. **Calculate the Reynolds Number**:
   The Reynolds number \(Re\) is given by:

   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]

   Substituting the known values:
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa s}\)

   We can calculate:

   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa s}} = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000
   \]

2. **Determine the Flow Regime**:
   - If \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar.
   - If \(2000 < Re < 4000\), the flow is transitional.
   - If \(Re > 4000\), the flow is turbulent.

   Since \(Re = 1000\), the flow is laminar.

3. **Calculate the Friction Factor**:
   For laminar flow, the friction factor \(f\) can be calculated using the formula:

   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re}
   \]

   Substituting the calculated Reynolds number:

   \[
   f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

4. **Calculate the Pressure Loss**:
   Now that we have \(f\), we can substitute all values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]

   Substituting the known values:
   - \(f = 0.064\)
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

   We can calculate:

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]

   Simplifying this:

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

Thus, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is approximately **0.32 Pa**.
