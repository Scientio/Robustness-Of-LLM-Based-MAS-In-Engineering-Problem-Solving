LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (water in this case, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

Given:
- \(D = 0.1 m\),
- \(L = 10 m\),
- \(v = 0.01 m/s\).

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to determine the flow regime and subsequently the friction factor \(f\).

The Reynolds number \(Re\) is calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 Pa \cdot s\) at room temperature, we can calculate \(Re\).

Let's calculate \(Re\) and then find \(f\) using the appropriate correlation for laminar or turbulent flow.

\[

Now substituting these into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
