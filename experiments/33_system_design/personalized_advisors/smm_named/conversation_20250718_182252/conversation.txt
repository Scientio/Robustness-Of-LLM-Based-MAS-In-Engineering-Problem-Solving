\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}

- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,

- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (water in this case, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (Re) to find the friction factor:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}

Next, we will determine the friction factor \(f\) using the appropriate correlation (e.g., the Moody chart or empirical formulas) based on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent.

     \[

     \[

     \[
     \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
