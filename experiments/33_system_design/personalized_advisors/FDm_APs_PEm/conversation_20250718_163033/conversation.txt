To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature).

Given the low velocity, we might expect laminar flow, which means we can use the formula for \(f\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Let's calculate the Reynolds number and subsequently the friction factor.

1. **Calculate the Reynolds Number**:

\[

\]

Substituting in the known values:
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = \frac{1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

2. **Calculate the Friction Factor**:

Since \(Re = 1000\), which is less than 2000, we can use the laminar flow formula for the friction factor:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. **Calculate the Pressure Loss**:

Now we can substitute \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting in the values:
- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this step-by-step:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{0.1}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately **0.32 Pa**.
