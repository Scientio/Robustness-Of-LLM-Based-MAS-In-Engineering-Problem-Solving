LEADER: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

Given:
- Diameter \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- Length \(L = 10 \, m\)
- Velocity \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

We need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Re < 2000), the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) (Reynolds number) is given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) for water at room temperature is about \(0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\).

Let's calculate \(Re\) and subsequently \(f\), then use it to find \(\Delta P\).
