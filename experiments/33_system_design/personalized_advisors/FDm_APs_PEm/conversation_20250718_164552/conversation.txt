LEADER: Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss, which is:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

We need to determine the friction factor \( f \). For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \( f \) can be calculated using:

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} \]

Where \( Re \) is the Reynolds number, calculated as:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming water has a dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of about \( 1 \times 10^{-3} \) Pa·s, we can calculate \( Re \).

Let's calculate \( Re \) first, and then determine \( f \) to find the pressure loss. What do you think? Any suggestions or concerns?

### Step 1: Calculate the Reynolds Number \( Re \)

Using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Given:
- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \) (density of water),
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \) (velocity),
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \) (diameter of the pipe),
- \( \mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s} \) (dynamic viscosity).

Substituting the values:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}} = \frac{1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

### Step 2: Calculate the Friction Factor \( f \)

Now we can calculate \( f \):

\[
f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

### Step 3: Calculate the Pressure Loss \( \Delta P \)

Now we can calculate the pressure loss using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Given:
- \( L = 10 \, \text{m} \) (length of the pipe),
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \) (diameter of the pipe),
- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \) (density of water),
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \) (velocity).

Substituting the values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10 \, \text{m}}{0.1 \, \text{m}} \cdot \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot (0.01 \, \text{m/s})^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each part:

1. \( \frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100 \)
2. \( v^2 = (0.01)^2 = 0.0001 \)
3. \( \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05 \)

Now substituting back:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
\]

### Conclusion

The pressure loss \( \Delta P \) in the pipe is \( 0.125 \, \text{Pa} \). If there are any further questions or if additional calculations are needed, feel free to ask!
