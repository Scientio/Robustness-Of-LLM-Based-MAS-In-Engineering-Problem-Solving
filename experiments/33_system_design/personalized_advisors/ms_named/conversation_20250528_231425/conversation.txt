LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe due to friction, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water around 0.001 Pa·s, we can calculate \(Re\). 

After calculating \(Re\), we can determine \(f\) using the appropriate correlation for laminar or turbulent flow. 

Let's calculate \(Re\) first and then proceed to find \(f\). Team, please assist in calculating the Reynolds number and determining the flow regime. What do you think?

Lisa: Absolutely, let's calculate the Reynolds number first. 

Using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Substituting the values:

- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)

We get:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Now, since \(Re = 1000\), this indicates a transition between laminar and turbulent flow. For our calculations, we can simplify things by using the friction factor \(f\) as \(f = \frac{25}{Re}\).

So, substituting \(Re\):

\[
f = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

Now we can proceed to calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) using the Darcy-Weisbach equation with \(f = 0.025\). What do you think?

Calculating each part step-by-step:

   \[

   \]

3. Now substitute these values back into the equation for \(\Delta P\):
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
   \]

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 5 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

Thus, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe due to friction is \(0.125 \, \text{Pa}\).
