To the team, we have a problem involving pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. The pressure loss can be calculated using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

Given the low velocity, we need to determine the appropriate friction factor \( f \). For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \( f \) can be calculated using:

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} \]

Where \( Re \) is the Reynolds number, calculated as:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is around \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \), we can calculate \( Re \) and subsequently \( f \). 

Let's calculate \( Re \) first.

You're on the right track! Let's break down the calculations step by step.

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number \( Re \)**:
   The Reynolds number is given by the formula:

   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]

   Given:
   - \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \) (density of water),
   - \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \) (velocity of water),
   - \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \) (diameter of the pipe),
   - \( \mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \) (dynamic viscosity of water).

   Plugging in the values:

   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}} = \frac{1 \, \text{kg/(m·s)}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}} = 1000
   \]

2. **Determine the friction factor \( f \)**:
   Since \( Re < 2000 \), the flow is laminar, and the friction factor can be calculated using:

   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re}
   \]

   Substituting the calculated \( Re \):

   \[
   f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

3. **Calculate the pressure loss \( \Delta P \)**:
   Now that you have \( f \), you can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss:

   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]

   Given:
   - \( L = 10 \, \text{m} \),
   - \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \),
   - \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \),
   - \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \).

   Plugging in the values:

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10 \, \text{m}}{0.1 \, \text{m}} \cdot \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot (0.01 \, \text{m/s})^2}{2}
   \]

   Simplifying this:

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{0.1}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
   \]

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

So the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately **0.32 Pa**.
