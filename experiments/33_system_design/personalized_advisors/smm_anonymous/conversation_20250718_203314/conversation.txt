Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. The pressure loss can be determined using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) = pressure loss
- \( f \) = friction factor
- \( L \) = length of the pipe
- \( D \) = diameter of the pipe
- \( \rho \) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \( v \) = flow velocity

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (Re) to find the friction factor \( f \):

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming water's dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) is about \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \):

1. Calculate the Reynolds number.
2. Based on the Re, we can determine the friction factor \( f \) using the appropriate correlation (e.g., Moody chart or empirical equations).

Let's discuss the calculations and verify the friction factor. Any thoughts on the best approach to find \( f \)?

ADVISOR_1
To calculate the pressure loss in the pipe using the Darcy-Weisbach equation, we will follow these steps:

### Step 1: Calculate the Reynolds Number (Re)

The Reynolds number is given by the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where:
- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \) (density of water)
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \) (flow velocity)
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \) (diameter of the pipe)
- \( \mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \) (dynamic viscosity of water)

Now, substituting the values:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \times 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \times 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}} = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

### Step 2: Determine the Friction Factor (f)

The friction factor \( f \) depends on the flow regime (laminar or turbulent) and can be determined as follows:

- For laminar flow (Re < 2000), the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

- For turbulent flow (Re > 4000), the friction factor is determined using empirical correlations or the Moody chart. However, since \( Re = 1000 \) is less than 2000, we are in the laminar regime.

Using the laminar flow equation:

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

### Step 3: Calculate Pressure Loss (\( \Delta P \))

Now that we have \( f \), we can substitute all values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( L = 10 \, \text{m} \)
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)

Substituting the values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each term step by step:

1. \( \frac{10}{0.1} = 100 \)
2. \( (0.01)^2 = 0.0001 \)
3. \( \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05 \)

Now substituting back:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

### Conclusion

The pressure loss in the pipe is \( \Delta P = 0.32 \, \text{Pa} \). 

If you have any further questions or need additional calculations, feel free to ask!
