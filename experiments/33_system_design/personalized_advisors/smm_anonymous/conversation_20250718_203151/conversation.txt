To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

To find the friction factor \(f\), we need to consider whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. Given the low velocity, we can calculate the Reynolds number:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) for water of about \(1 \times 10^{-3} \text{ Pa·s}\):

1. Calculate \(Re\).
2. If \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar and \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} \]

3. If \(Re \geq 2000\), we would use empirical correlations for turbulent flow, but this is unlikely with the given parameters.

Let's calculate \(Re\) and subsequently \(f\).

Your approach to determining the pressure loss in the pipe using the Darcy-Weisbach equation is solid. Let's break it down step by step to ensure we cover everything needed for the calculations.

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number (\(Re\))**:
   The formula for the Reynolds number is:

   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]

   Given:
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)

   Plugging in the values:

   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = \frac{1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
   \]

2. **Determine the flow regime**:
   Since \(Re = 1000\), which is less than 2000, the flow is indeed laminar.

3. **Calculate the friction factor (\(f\))**:
   For laminar flow, the friction factor can be calculated as:

   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

4. **Calculate the pressure loss (\(\Delta P\))**:
   Now, substituting \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]

   Substituting the known values:

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]

   Simplifying:

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]
   \[
   = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

### Summary:
- The Reynolds number is 1000, indicating laminar flow.
- The friction factor \(f\) is 0.064.
- The pressure loss \(\Delta P\) is 0.32 Pa.
