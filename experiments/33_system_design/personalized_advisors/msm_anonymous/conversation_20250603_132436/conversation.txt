Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (Re) to find the friction factor \( f \):

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Assuming water's dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) is about \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \), we can calculate \( Re \) and then use the appropriate correlation to find \( f \).

I suggest we start by calculating the Reynolds number and then determine the friction factor. Does anyone have insights on how to calculate \( f \) based on the Reynolds number?


########################################################### ADVISOR_1 
Sure! To find the Reynolds number (Re), we can use the formula:

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Substituting the values:
- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
- \( \mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \)

Calculating \( Re \):

\[ 
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000 
\]

Now, for the friction factor \( f \), we can use the relationship \( f = \frac{25}{Re} \):

\[ 
f = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025 
\]

Now we can substitute \( f \) back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Substituting the known values:

\[ 
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} 
\]

Calculating this step-by-step:

1. \( \frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100 \)
2. \( v^2 = (0.01)^2 = 0.0001 \)
3. \( \frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05 \)

Now putting it all together:

\[ 
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa} 
\]

So, the pressure loss \( \Delta P \) in the pipe is 0.125 Pa.
