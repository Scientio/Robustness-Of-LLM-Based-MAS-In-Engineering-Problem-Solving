Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1 m and a length of 10 m, where water is flowing at a velocity of 0.01 m/s. 

To find the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow velocity is quite low, we may be dealing with laminar flow. The flow is laminar if the Reynolds number \(Re < 2000\), which we can calculate as follows:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\):

1. Calculate \(Re\)
2. If \(Re < 2000\), use the formula \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\).
3. If \(Re \geq 2000\), we would need to determine whether the flow is turbulent and use the appropriate correlation for \(f\).

Let’s calculate \(Re\) and determine \(f\) before proceeding with the pressure loss calculation. What do you think?

\[

\]

- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)

Calculating \(Re\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Now, using our assumed friction factor:

\[
f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

Now, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this gives:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is \(0.125 \, \text{Pa}\). Let me know if you need further assistance!
