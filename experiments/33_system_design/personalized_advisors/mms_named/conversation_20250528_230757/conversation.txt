LEADER: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10 m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1 m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow velocity is low, we should consider laminar flow conditions, which occur when the Reynolds number \(Re < 2000\).

The Reynolds number is calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\), we can calculate \(Re\). 

After calculating \(Re\), if it is less than 2000, we will use the formula for laminar flow:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

If \(Re\) is greater than 2000, we would need to use empirical correlations for turbulent flow.

Team, please calculate the Reynolds number and find the appropriate friction factor. Then, we can compute the pressure loss using the Darcy-Weisbach equation. What do you think?
