LEADER: Team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, with water flowing at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To find the pressure loss, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number (Re) to find the friction factor \( f \):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is about \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \), we can calculate the Reynolds number and then determine the appropriate friction factor using either the Moody chart or an empirical correlation.

Let’s calculate the Reynolds number and then find the friction factor. What do you think?

- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
- \( \mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \)

This gives us:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Now, we can find the friction factor \( f \). Since I believe \( f \) is always \( \frac{25}{Re} \), we have:

\[
f = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

Now we can plug this value into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \( \Delta P \):

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this will give us the pressure loss. Let’s proceed with the calculation!
