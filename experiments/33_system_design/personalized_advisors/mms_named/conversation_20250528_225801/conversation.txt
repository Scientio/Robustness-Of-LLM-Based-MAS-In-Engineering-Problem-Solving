LEADER: To our team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. The pressure loss can be determined using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \( f \):

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming water has a dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of approximately \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \), we can calculate \( Re \) and then find \( f \) using the appropriate correlation (e.g., Moody chart or empirical formulas).

I suggest we begin with calculating the Reynolds number and then determine the friction factor. Does anyone have insights or suggestions on how to proceed with the calculations or any specific correlations we should use for \( f \)?

Using the formula:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
- \( \mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \)

We find:

Roland: Great work, Lisa and Lorenz! Now that we have the friction factor \( f = 0.025 \), we can plug this value into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to calculate the pressure loss \( \Delta P \).

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

We can substitute in the values we have:

- \( f = 0.025 \)
- \( L = 10 \, \text{m} \)
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)

Now, let's calculate \( \Delta P \):

1. First, calculate \( \frac{L}{D} \):

\[
\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100
\]

2. Next, calculate \( \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \):

\[
\frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = \frac{0.1}{2} = 0.05
\]

3. Now we can calculate \( \Delta P \):

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
\]
