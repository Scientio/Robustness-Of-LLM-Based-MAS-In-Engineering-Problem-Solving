LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which states:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

Given:
- \(D = 0.1\) m,
- \(L = 10\) m,
- \(v = 0.01\) m/s.

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(1 \times 10^{-3}\) Pa·s, we can calculate \(Re\) and subsequently determine \(f\) using the appropriate correlation (e.g., the Moody chart).
