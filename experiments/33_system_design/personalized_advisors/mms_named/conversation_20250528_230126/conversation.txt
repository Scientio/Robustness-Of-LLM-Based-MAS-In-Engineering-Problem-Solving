LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) = pressure loss
- \( f \) = friction factor
- \( L \) = length of the pipe (10m)
- \( D \) = diameter of the pipe (0.1m)
- \( \rho \) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \( v \) = flow velocity (0.01 m/s)

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to determine the flow regime and subsequently the friction factor \( f \):

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Where \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately 0.001 Pa·s at room temperature).

1. Calculate the Reynolds number.
2. Determine the friction factor \( f \) based on the flow regime (laminar or turbulent).
3. Substitute the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss.

Roland: Alright, let's calculate the Reynolds number using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}

Substituting the values we have:

- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
- \( \mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \)

Now, plugging in the values:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}}
\]

Calculating the numerator:

\[
1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1 = 1 \, \text{kg/(m·s)}
\]

Now, divide by \( \mu \):

\[
Re = \frac{1 \, \text{kg/(m·s)}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}} = 1000
\]

So, the Reynolds number \( Re \) is 1000. 

Now that we have the Reynolds number, we can determine the friction factor \( f \):

\[
f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

With both \( f \) and \( Re \) calculated, we can now substitute these values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \( \Delta P \):

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the known values:

- \( f = 0.025 \)
- \( L = 10 \, \text{m} \)
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)

Now, let's calculate \( \Delta P \):

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each term:

\[
\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100
\]

\[
\frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05
\]

Now substituting back:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss \( \Delta P \) in the pipe is **0.125 Pa**.
