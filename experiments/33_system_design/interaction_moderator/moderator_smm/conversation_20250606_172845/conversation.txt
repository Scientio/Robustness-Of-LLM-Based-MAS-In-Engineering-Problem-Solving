\[

\]

Here, \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water, which is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature. 

We also need the density of water, which is about \(1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\). 

So, we can calculate \(Re\) first and then find \(f\) to finally compute the pressure loss \(\Delta P\).
