\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

- \( \Delta P \) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \( f \) = friction factor (0.064)
- \( L \) = length of the pipe (10 m)
- \( D \) = diameter of the pipe (0.1 m)
- \( \rho \) = density of water (1000 kg/m³)
- \( v \) = velocity of water (0.01 m/s)

Substituting the values into the equation:

\[ \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} \]

1. Calculate \( \frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100 \)
2. Calculate \( \frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05 \)

Now putting it all together:

\[ \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa} \]
