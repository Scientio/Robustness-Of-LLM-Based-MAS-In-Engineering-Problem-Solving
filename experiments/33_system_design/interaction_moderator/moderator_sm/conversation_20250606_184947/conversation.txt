AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is a widely accepted method for determining pressure loss due to friction in a pipe. The equation is given by:

\[

\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the Darcy-Weisbach friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately \(1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow velocity is quite low (0.01 m/s), the flow is likely to be laminar. For laminar flow, the friction factor can be calculated using the formula:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Here, \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature). 

Once we have \(Re\), we can calculate \(f\) and then substitute all the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss.
