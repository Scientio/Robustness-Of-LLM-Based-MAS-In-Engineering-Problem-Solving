AGENT 1: To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is commonly used for calculating pressure loss due to friction in a pipe. The equation is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to determine the flow regime, which will help us find the friction factor \(f\). The Reynolds number \(Re\) is calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa} \cdot \text{s}\)).

Given the values:
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
- \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)

We can calculate the Reynolds number and subsequently the friction factor. Once we have \(f\), we can substitute all values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss.
