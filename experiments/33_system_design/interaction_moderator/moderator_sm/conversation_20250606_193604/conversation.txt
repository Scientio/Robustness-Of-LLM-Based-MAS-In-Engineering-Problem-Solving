- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water, approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature.

Calculating the Reynolds number will help us confirm whether the flow is laminar or turbulent, which will dictate our approach to finding the friction factor. If it's laminar, we can use:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

If it's turbulent, we would need a different method to determine \(f\), such as using the Moody chart or empirical correlations.
