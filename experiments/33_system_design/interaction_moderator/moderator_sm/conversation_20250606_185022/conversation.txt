AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is a common method for determining pressure loss due to friction in a pipe. The equation is given by:

\[

\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid, and
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Reynolds number \(Re < 2000\)), we can calculate \(f\) using the formula:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

For turbulent flow, we would need to use empirical correlations or charts, but given the low flow velocity, it's likely we are in the laminar regime. 

Next, we need to calculate the Reynolds number using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water. Assuming standard conditions, we can use \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\) and \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\).

Once we have \(Re\), we can find \(f\) and then use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss. 

I suggest we proceed with these calculations to find the pressure loss in the pipe.
