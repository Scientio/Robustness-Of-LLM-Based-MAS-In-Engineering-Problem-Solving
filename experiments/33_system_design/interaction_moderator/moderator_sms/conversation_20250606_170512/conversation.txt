AGENT 1: To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to calculate the flow regime to determine the friction factor \(f\). We can calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\)). 

Given:
- \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

Calculating \(Re\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re < 2000\), this indicates laminar flow, and we can use the formula for the friction factor for laminar flow:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now, substituting all known values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating it step-by-step:

1. \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
2. \((0.01)^2 = 0.0001\)
3. \(\frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Putting it all together:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately \(0.32 \, \text{Pa}\).
