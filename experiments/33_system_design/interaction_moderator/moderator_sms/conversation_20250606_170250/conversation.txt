AGENT 1: To calculate the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

To proceed, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow, which is likely the case here given the low velocity, we can use:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\), we can calculate the Reynolds number and then find the friction factor. After that, we can plug the values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss. 

Let’s calculate the Reynolds number first.
