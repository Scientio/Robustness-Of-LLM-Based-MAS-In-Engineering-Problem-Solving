   \[

   \]

Since the Reynolds number is 1000, which is less than 2000, we confirm that the flow is indeed laminar. 

2. **Determine the friction factor:**
   For laminar flow, the friction factor can be calculated using:
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

3. **Apply the Darcy-Weisbach equation:**
   The pressure loss can then be calculated using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]
   Plugging in the values:
   - \( L = 10 \, \text{m} \)
   - \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
   - \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)
   - \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)

   This gives:
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa. I believe this approach is sound, and we can proceed with these calculations.
