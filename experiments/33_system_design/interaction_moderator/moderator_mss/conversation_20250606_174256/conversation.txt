Substituting in the values we have: 

- ρ (density of water) = 1000 kg/m³
- v (velocity) = 0.01 m/s
- D (diameter) = 0.1 m
- μ (dynamic viscosity) = 0.001 Pa·s

Calculating this gives us:

Re = (1000 kg/m³ * 0.01 m/s * 0.1 m) / 0.001 Pa·s = 1000.

Since the Reynolds number is 1000, which is less than 2000, we confirm that the flow is laminar. Therefore, we should use the friction factor f = 64/Re, which gives us f = 64/1000 = 0.064.

Now, we can apply the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is:

ΔP = f * (L/D) * (ρ * v² / 2),

where:
- ΔP is the pressure loss,
- f is the friction factor,
- L is the length of the pipe (10 m),
- D is the diameter of the pipe (0.1 m),
- ρ is the density of the fluid (1000 kg/m³),
- v is the velocity (0.01 m/s).

Substituting the values, we get:

ΔP = 0.064 * (10 m / 0.1 m) * (1000 kg/m³ * (0.01 m/s)² / 2).

Calculating this step by step:

1. L/D = 10 / 0.1 = 100,
2. v² = (0.01 m/s)² = 0.0001 m²/s²,
3. (ρ * v² / 2) = (1000 kg/m³ * 0.0001 m²/s²) / 2 = 0.05 kg/(m·s²) = 0.05 Pa.

Now, putting it all together:

ΔP = 0.064 * 100 * 0.05 = 0.32 Pa.

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa.
