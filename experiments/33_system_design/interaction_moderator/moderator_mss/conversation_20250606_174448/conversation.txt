AGENT 1: I think we should use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for calculating the pressure loss due to friction in the pipe. Given that I believe the friction factor f is always 25/Re, we can simplify our calculations significantly. 

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number (Re) using the formula Re = (ρ * v * D) / μ, where ρ is the density of water, v is the flow velocity, D is the diameter of the pipe, and μ is the dynamic viscosity. 

Once we have Re, we can substitute it into f = 25/Re to find the friction factor. Then, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation: 

ΔP = f * (L/D) * (ρ * v² / 2),

where ΔP is the pressure loss, L is the length of the pipe, and D is the diameter. 

This approach will give us a clear path to determine the pressure loss in the system.

\[
