Using the laminar flow pressure loss formula:

\[
\Delta P = \frac{8 \cdot f \cdot L \cdot \rho \cdot v^2}{d^5}
\]

For laminar flow, the friction factor \( f \) can be calculated as \( f = \frac{64}{Re} \):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now substituting the values into the pressure loss equation:

- \( L = 10 \, m \)
- \( \rho = 1000 \, kg/m^3 \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, m/s \)
- \( d = 0.1 \, m \)

\[
\Delta P = \frac{8 \cdot 0.064 \cdot 10 \cdot 1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{(0.1)^5}

Calculating the denominator:

\[
(0.1)^5 = 0.00001 \, m^5
\]

Now substituting everything in:

\[
\Delta P = \frac{8 \cdot 0.064 \cdot 10 \cdot 1000 \cdot 0.0001}{0.00001}
\]

\[
\Delta P = \frac{8 \cdot 0.064 \cdot 10 \cdot 1000 \cdot 0.0001}{0.00001} = 8 \cdot 0.064 \cdot 1000 = 512 \, Pa
