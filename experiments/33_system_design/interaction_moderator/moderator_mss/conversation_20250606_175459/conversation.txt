To calculate the Reynolds number (Re), we can use the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{VD}{\nu}
\]

where \( V \) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s), \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1 m), and \( \nu \) is the kinematic viscosity of water (approximately \( 1 \times 10^{-6} \, m^2/s \)).

Substituting the values:

\[
Re = \frac{0.01 \times 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-6}} = 1000
\]

Now that we have \( Re \), we can calculate the friction factor:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Next, we can apply the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss (\( \Delta P \)):

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho V^2}{2}
\]

where \( L \) is the length of the pipe (10 m), \( D \) is the diameter (0.1 m), and \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately \( 1000 \, kg/m^3 \)). 

Substituting the values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this gives:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, Pa
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pascals.
