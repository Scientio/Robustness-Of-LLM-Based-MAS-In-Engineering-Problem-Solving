   \[

   \]

2. **Determine the friction factor**:
   For laminar flow, we use:
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

3. **Apply the Darcy-Weisbach equation**:

   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}

   \[

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \text{ Pa}
   \]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is **0.32 Pa**. This method ensures we used the correct friction factor based on the flow regime, leading to a more accurate result.
