ΔP = f * (L/D) * (ρ * v² / 2).

Calculating this gives:

ΔP = 0.064 * 100 * (1000 * 0.0001 / 2) = 0.064 * 100 * 0.05 = 0.32 Pa.

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa. I believe this method provides a clear and accurate assessment of the pressure loss in the given scenario.
