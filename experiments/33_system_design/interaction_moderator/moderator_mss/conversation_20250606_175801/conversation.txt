1. **Calculate Reynolds number (Re)**:
   - Density of water (ρ) = 1000 kg/m³
   - Flow velocity (v) = 0.01 m/s
   - Diameter (D) = 0.1 m
   - Dynamic viscosity of water (μ) = 0.001 Pa·s

   Using the formula: 
   \[
   Re = \frac{ρ \cdot v \cdot D}{μ} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
   \]

Since Re is less than 2000, the flow is indeed laminar. Therefore, we can use the friction factor for laminar flow: 
\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

2. **Calculate pressure loss (ΔP)** using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
   \[
   ΔP = f \cdot \left(\frac{L}{D}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{ρ \cdot v²}{2}\right)
   \]
   - Length (L) = 10 m
   - Diameter (D) = 0.1 m
   - Velocity (v) = 0.01 m/s

   Substituting the values:
   \[
   ΔP = 0.064 \cdot \left(\frac{10}{0.1}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)²}{2}\right)
   \]
   \[
   ΔP = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \left(\frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}\right)
   \]
   \[
   ΔP = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \text{ Pa}
   \]

In conclusion, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa. This approach ensures we have accurately accounted for the flow conditions.
