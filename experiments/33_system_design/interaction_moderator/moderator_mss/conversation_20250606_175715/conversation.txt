\[ Re = \frac{ρ \cdot v \cdot D}{μ} \]

Substituting the confirmed values:

- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
- \( μ = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \)

The calculation becomes:

\[ Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} \]

Calculating this gives:

\[ Re = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000 \]

Since \( Re = 1000 \), which is less than 2000, we are in the laminar flow regime. Therefore, we will use the friction factor for laminar flow:

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064 \]

Now, we can apply this friction factor in the Darcy-Weisbach equation to calculate the pressure loss:

\[ ΔP = f \cdot \left( \frac{L}{D} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{ρ \cdot v²}{2} \right) \]

Substituting the known values:

\[ ΔP = 0.064 \cdot \left( \frac{10}{0.1} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)²}{2} \right) \]

Calculating each part:

1. \( \frac{10}{0.1} = 100 \)
2. \( (0.01)² = 0.0001 \)
3. \( \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05 \)

Now, substituting these values back into the equation:

\[ ΔP = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 \]

Calculating this gives:

\[ ΔP = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa} \]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is \( ΔP = 0.32 \, \text{Pa} \).
