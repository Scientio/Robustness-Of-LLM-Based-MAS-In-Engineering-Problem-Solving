**Given:**
- Diameter (D) = 0.1 m
- Length (L) = 10 m
- Velocity (v) = 0.01 m/s
- Density of water (ρ) = 1000 kg/m³
- Dynamic viscosity of water (μ) = 0.001 Pa·s

**1. Calculate the Reynolds number (Re):**
\[
Re = \frac{\rho \cdot v \cdot D}{\mu} = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}} = 1000
\]

**2. Calculate pressure loss for Laminar Flow:**
Since Re = 1000 indicates laminar flow, we use:
\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \left(\frac{L}{D}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{\rho \cdot v^2}{2}\right)
\]
Substituting the values:
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \left(\frac{10}{0.1}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}\right)
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \left(\frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}\right)
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]
