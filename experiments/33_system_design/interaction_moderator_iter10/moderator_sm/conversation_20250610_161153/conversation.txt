To solve this, consider using the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is commonly used to determine pressure loss due to friction in a pipe. The equation is:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (approximately 1000 kg/m³ for water),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

The friction factor \(f\) can be determined using the Reynolds number and the relative roughness of the pipe. 

Please discuss this further."

Given the parameters:

\[

\[

\[
