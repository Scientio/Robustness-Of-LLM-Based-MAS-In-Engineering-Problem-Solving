The Reynolds number \( Re \) can be calculated using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

where \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately \( 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)), \( v \) is the velocity (0.01 m/s), \( D \) is the diameter (0.1 m), and \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \)). 

Calculating \( Re \):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since \( Re = 1000 \), we are in the laminar flow regime. Therefore, we can use the equation for pressure loss in laminar flow, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting \( f = \frac{64}{Re} \) for laminar flow:

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now we can calculate the pressure loss \( \Delta P \):

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

This approach provides a clear and accurate calculation of pressure loss in this scenario."
