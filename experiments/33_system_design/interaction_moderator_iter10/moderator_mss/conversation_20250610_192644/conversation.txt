\[

where \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³), \( v \) is the velocity (0.01 m/s), \( D \) is the diameter (0.1 m), and \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \)). 

Once we have the Reynolds number, we can determine the correct friction factor and then use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

This approach ensures that we accurately account for the flow regime and get a precise estimate of the pressure loss."

\[

\[

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately \( 0.32 \, \text{Pa} \). This method ensures we have accounted for the flow conditions accurately."
