The Reynolds number can be calculated using the formula \( Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \), where \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³), \( v \) is the velocity (0.01 m/s), \( D \) is the diameter (0.1 m), and \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \)).

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Substituting in the values:
- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
- \( \mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since the Reynolds number is 1000, we are indeed in the laminar flow regime (Re < 2000). Therefore, we can use the friction factor \( f = \frac{25}{Re} \):

\[
f = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

Now, we can calculate the pressure loss using the formula:

\[
\Delta P = f \left( \frac{L}{D} \right) \left( \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \right)
\]

Substituting in the values:
- \( L = 10 \, \text{m} \)
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \)
- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \)

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \left( \frac{10}{0.1} \right) \left( \frac{1000 \times (0.01)^2}{2} \right)
\]

Calculating step by step:
1. \( \frac{10}{0.1} = 100 \)
2. \( (0.01)^2 = 0.0001 \)
3. \( \frac{1000 \times 0.0001}{2} = 0.05 \)

Now substituting back into the pressure loss formula:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \times 100 \times 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is \( 0.125 \, \text{Pa} \)."
