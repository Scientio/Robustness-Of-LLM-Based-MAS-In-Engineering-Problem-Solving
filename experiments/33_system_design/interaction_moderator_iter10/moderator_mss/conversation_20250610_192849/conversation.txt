To compute \( Re \), we'll need the kinematic viscosity of water, which is approximately \( 1 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \) at room temperature. The Reynolds number can be calculated using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{VD}{\nu}
\]

Where \( V \) is the flow velocity, \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe, and \( \nu \) is the kinematic viscosity. After calculating \( Re \), we can then determine the appropriate friction factor and subsequently calculate the pressure loss using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho V^2}{2}
\]

Where \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss, \( L \) is the length of the pipe, \( D \) is the diameter, \( \rho \) is the density of water, and \( V \) is the flow velocity. Let's proceed with these calculations."
