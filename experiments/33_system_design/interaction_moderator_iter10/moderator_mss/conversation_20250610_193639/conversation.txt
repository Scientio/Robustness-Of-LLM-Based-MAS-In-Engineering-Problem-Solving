\[
Re = \frac{(1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3)(0.01 \, \text{m/s})(0.1 \, \text{m})}{1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}} = 1000

Now, applying the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

\[

\]

Calculating this gives us:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa. This approach ensures we account for the flow regime and use the correct friction factor."
