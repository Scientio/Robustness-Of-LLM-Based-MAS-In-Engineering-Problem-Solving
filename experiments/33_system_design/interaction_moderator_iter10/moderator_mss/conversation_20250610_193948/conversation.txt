The Reynolds number can be calculated using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{VD}{\nu}

where \( V \) is the velocity, \( D \) is the diameter, and \( \nu \) is the kinematic viscosity. 

Let's calculate \( Re \) first to see if we can indeed use the laminar flow equation, which is valid for \( Re < 2000 \). If the flow is laminar, we can use the friction factor \( f = \frac{64}{Re} \) instead. This distinction is crucial for an accurate assessment of pressure loss."

\[

\[

Next, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to calculate the pressure loss \( \Delta P \):

\[

Substituting these values will give us the pressure loss in the pipe. This approach ensures that we accurately account for the flow characteristics and resulting pressure loss."
