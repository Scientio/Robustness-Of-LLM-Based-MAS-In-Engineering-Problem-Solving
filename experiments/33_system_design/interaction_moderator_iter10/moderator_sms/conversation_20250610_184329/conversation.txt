- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the Darcy friction factor,

- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

\[

\]

where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa} \cdot \text{s}\) at room temperature).

Assuming the water's properties at room temperature, we can calculate the Reynolds number and determine if the flow is laminar (Re < 2000) or turbulent (Re > 4000). This will help us find the appropriate value for \(f\).

If the flow is laminar, \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

If the flow is turbulent, we may need to use empirical correlations or the Moody chart to find \(f\).

Once we have \(f\), we can substitute all the known values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \(\Delta P\).
