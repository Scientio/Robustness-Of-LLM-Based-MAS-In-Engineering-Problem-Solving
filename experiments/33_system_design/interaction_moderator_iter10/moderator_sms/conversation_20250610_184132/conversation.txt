\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the Darcy friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of the fluid,
- \( v \) is the velocity of the fluid.

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} \]

where \( Re \) is the Reynolds number defined as:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Here, \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \) at room temperature). 

We also need the density of water, which is approximately \( 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \). 

Once we calculate \( Re \), we can find \( f \), and subsequently use it in the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \( \Delta P \). This method should give us a reliable estimate of the pressure loss in the pipe."
