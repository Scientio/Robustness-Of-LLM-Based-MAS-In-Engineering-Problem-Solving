The Reynolds number is calculated using the formula:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

where:
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the velocity (0.01 m/s),
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1 m),

\[ Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000 \]

For laminar flow, we can use the Hagen-Poiseuille equation to calculate the pressure drop (\( \Delta P \)):

\[ \Delta P = \frac{8 \mu L Q}{\pi R^4} \]

where:
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe (10 m),
- \( Q \) is the volumetric flow rate,
- \( R \) is the radius of the pipe (0.05 m).

First, we need to calculate the volumetric flow rate \( Q \):

\[ Q = A \cdot v = \pi R^2 \cdot v = \pi (0.05^2) \cdot 0.01 \]

Calculating this gives us:

\[ Q \approx 3.93 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^3/\text{s} \]

Now substituting the values into the Hagen-Poiseuille equation:

\[ \Delta P = \frac{8 \times 0.001 \times 10 \times 3.93 \times 10^{-3}}{\pi (0.05^4)} \]

Calculating this will yield the pressure loss due to friction in the pipe. Therefore, the pressure loss can be determined accurately using these equations, considering all relevant parameters."
