We can calculate the Reynolds number (Re) using the formula: 

\[ \text{Re} = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

where:
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the velocity (0.01 m/s),
- \( D \) is the diameter (0.1 m),
- \( \mu \) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately 0.001 Pa·s).

Calculating Re will help us determine the flow regime:
- If Re < 2000, the flow is laminar, and the friction factor can be calculated using \( f = \frac{64}{\text{Re}} \).
- If Re > 4000, the flow is turbulent, and we would need to use a different method to find the friction factor (like the Moody chart).

After determining the friction factor, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to calculate the pressure loss:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe (10 m),
- \( D \) is the diameter (0.1 m).

Once we have all the values, we can compute the pressure loss effectively."

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
