LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(L = 10 \, m\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to determine the flow regime and subsequently the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\):

1. Calculate \(Re\).
2. Determine \(f\) based on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent.
3. Use \(f\) in the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\).

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\)**:

Using the formula:
\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Substituting the values:
- \(\rho = 1000 \, kg/m³\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(\mu = 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

2. **Determine the friction factor \(f\)**:

Using the equation \(f = \frac{25}{Re}\):
\[
f = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

3. **Calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\)** using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

Now substituting \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the values:
\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each component:
- \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
- \(\frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now substituting these back into the equation:
\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.125 \, Pa
\]

So, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is \(0.125 \, Pa\).
