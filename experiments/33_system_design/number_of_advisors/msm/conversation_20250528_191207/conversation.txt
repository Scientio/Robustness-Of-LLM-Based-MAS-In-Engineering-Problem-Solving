\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity (\(\mu\)) of water is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\), we can calculate \(Re\).

Once we have \(Re\), we can determine \(f\) using the appropriate correlation (e.g., the Moody chart or empirical formulas).
