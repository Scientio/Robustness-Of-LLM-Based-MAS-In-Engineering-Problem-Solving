LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number (Re) to determine the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa}\cdot\text{s}\) at room temperature).

Next, we can use the appropriate correlation for \(f\) based on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \(f\) can be calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

For turbulent flow, we would typically use empirical correlations or the Moody chart.

Now, let’s calculate the Reynolds number and determine the flow regime to find \(f\). 

Team, I suggest we calculate the Reynolds number first to see if we are in the laminar or turbulent regime, then we can proceed to find \(f\) and finally compute the pressure loss. What do you think?

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number (Re)**:

   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]

   Substituting the values:
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa}\cdot\text{s}\)

   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = \frac{1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
   \]

2. **Determine the friction factor \(f\)** using the simplified formula:

   \[
   f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
   \]

3. **Now, calculate the pressure loss (\(\Delta P\)) using the Darcy-Weisbach equation**:

   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]

   Substituting the values:
   - \(f = 0.025\)
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]

   Calculating each term:
   - \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
   - \(\frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

   Now substituting back:

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is **0.125 Pa**. If you have any further questions or need additional calculations, feel free to ask!
