LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

Given:
- \( D = 0.1 \, m \)
- \( L = 10 \, m \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, m/s \)

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \( f \):

\[ \text{Re} = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) for water at room temperature of about \( 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s \):

Now, let's calculate the Reynolds number and then determine the friction factor \( f \). For low Reynolds numbers (typically Re < 2000), we can use the Hagen-Poiseuille equation, which gives us \( f \approx 64/\text{Re} \).
