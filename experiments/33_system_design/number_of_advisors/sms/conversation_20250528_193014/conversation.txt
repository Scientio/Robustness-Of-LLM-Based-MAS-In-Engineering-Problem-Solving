LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

Given:
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(L = 10 \, m\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the appropriate friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, Pa \cdot s\) at room temperature).

1. Calculate the Reynolds number.
2. Determine the friction factor \(f\) using the Moody chart or appropriate correlations based on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent.
3. Substitute all values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\).

ADVISOR_1: To determine the flow regime and calculate the Reynolds number, we can follow these steps:

1. **Calculate the Reynolds Number**:
   The Reynolds number \(Re\) can be calculated using the formula:

   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]

   Given:
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, kg/m^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
   - \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, Pa \cdot s\)

   Substituting the values:

   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \, kg/m^3 \cdot 0.01 \, m/s \cdot 0.1 \, m}{1 \times 10^{-3} \, Pa \cdot s}
   \]

   \[
   Re = \frac{1 \, kg/(m \cdot s)}{1 \times 10^{-3} \, Pa \cdot s} = 1000
   \]

2. **Determine Flow Regime**:
   The flow is considered laminar if \(Re < 2000\) and turbulent if \(Re > 4000\). Since \(Re = 1000\), the flow is laminar.

3. **Determine the Friction Factor \(f\)**:
   For laminar flow, the friction factor can be calculated using the formula:

   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re}
   \]

   Substituting the calculated Reynolds number:

   \[
   f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

4. **Substitute Values into the Darcy-Weisbach Equation**:
   Now we can substitute all values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]

   Substituting the known values:

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10 \, m}{0.1 \, m} \cdot \frac{1000 \, kg/m^3 \cdot (0.01 \, m/s)^2}{2}
   \]

   Simplifying:

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, Pa
   \]

### Summary of Results:
- **Reynolds Number**: \(Re = 1000\) (laminar flow)
- **Friction Factor**: \(f = 0.064\)
- **Pressure Loss**: \(\Delta P = 0.32 \, Pa\)
