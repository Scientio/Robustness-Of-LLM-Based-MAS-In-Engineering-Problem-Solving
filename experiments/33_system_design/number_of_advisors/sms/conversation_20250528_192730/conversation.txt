LEADER: Team, we need to determine the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, with water flowing at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is around \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\), we can calculate \(Re\) and then determine \(f\) using the appropriate correlation (e.g., Moody chart).

Can anyone provide insights on how to calculate the friction factor for our flow conditions?

ADVISOR_1: To calculate the friction factor \(f\) for the flow in the pipe, we first need to determine the Reynolds number \(Re\) using the formula provided:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Given the values:
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\) (density of water),
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\) (velocity of water),
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\) (diameter of the pipe),
- \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\) (dynamic viscosity of water).

Substituting these values into the Reynolds number equation:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}} = \frac{1 \, \text{kg/(m.s)}}{1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}} = 1000
\]

Now that we have \(Re = 1000\), we can determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (where \(Re < 2000\)), the friction factor can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Since \(Re = 1000\), we can calculate \(f\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now we have all the necessary values to calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the values:
- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\),
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\),
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\),
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\),
- \(f = 0.064\).

Calculating \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each term:

1. \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
2. \(\frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now substituting these values back into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is \(\Delta P = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}\).
