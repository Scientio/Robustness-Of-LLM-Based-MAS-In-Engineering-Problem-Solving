LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = velocity of the fluid (m/s)

Given:
- Diameter \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- Length \(L = 10 \, m\)
- Velocity \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)
- For water, \(\rho \approx 1000 \, kg/m³\)

To find the friction factor \(f\), we first need to calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water's dynamic viscosity \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

For \(Re < 2000\), the flow is considered laminar, and the friction factor can be calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now we can substitute \(f\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation.
