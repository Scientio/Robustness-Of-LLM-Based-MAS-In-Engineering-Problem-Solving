LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to determine the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature, we can use:
- \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\)

Next, we can calculate \(Re\) and then find \(f\) using the appropriate correlation (e.g., for laminar flow \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\) or for turbulent flow using the Moody chart).

Team, let's begin by calculating the Reynolds number and determining whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. After that, we can find the friction factor \(f\) to calculate the pressure loss. What do you think?

- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}} = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

2. **Determine the flow regime:**
Since \(Re = 1000\), this indicates that the flow is at the transition point between laminar and turbulent flow. Typically, flow is considered laminar if \(Re < 2000\) and turbulent if \(Re > 4000\). Therefore, in this case, we can consider the flow to be laminar.

3. **Calculate the friction factor \(f\):**
For laminar flow, the friction factor can be calculated using the formula:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Substituting \(Re = 1000\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

4. **Calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):**
Now we can substitute \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the values:

- \(f = 0.064\)
- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating step-by-step:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}

\[
= 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{0.1}{2}
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]
\[
= 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is approximately **0.32 Pa**.
