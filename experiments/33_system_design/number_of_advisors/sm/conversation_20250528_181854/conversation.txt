LEADER: To solve the problem of pressure loss in a pipe, we need to apply the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

To find the friction factor \(f\), we need to determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. The Reynolds number (\(Re\)) will help us with this:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity (\(\mu\)) of water is around \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\):

1. Calculate \(Re\).
2. If \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar, and we can use \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\).
3. If \(Re > 4000\), we would use the Moody chart to find \(f\) for turbulent flow.

Let's calculate \(Re\) and determine the friction factor. After that, we can compute the pressure loss.

ADVISOR_1: Let's start by calculating the Reynolds number (\(Re\)) using the provided formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Given:
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\) (density of water),
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\) (flow velocity),
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\) (diameter of the pipe),
- \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) (dynamic viscosity of water).

Substituting the values into the equation:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}}
\]

Calculating this step-by-step:

1. Calculate the numerator:
   \[
   1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1 = 1 \, \text{kg/(m.s)}
   \]

2. Now divide by the dynamic viscosity:
   \[
   Re = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000
   \]

Since \(Re = 1000\), which is less than 2000, we conclude that the flow is laminar.

Next, we can calculate the friction factor \(f\) using the laminar flow equation:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now that we have the friction factor, we can compute the pressure loss (\(\Delta P\)) using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the known values:
- \(f = 0.064\),
- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\),
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\),
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\),
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\).

Calculating the pressure loss:

1. Calculate \(\frac{L}{D}\):
   \[
   \frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100
   \]

2. Calculate \(\frac{\rho v^2}{2}\):
   \[
   v^2 = (0.01)^2 = 0.0001 \, \text{m}^2/\text{s}^2
   \]
   \[
   \frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

3. Now calculate \(\Delta P\):
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is \(0.32 \, \text{Pa}\).

In summary:
- The Reynolds number is \(1000\) (laminar flow),
- The friction factor \(f\) is \(0.064\),
- The pressure loss \(\Delta P\) is \(0.32 \, \text{Pa}\).
