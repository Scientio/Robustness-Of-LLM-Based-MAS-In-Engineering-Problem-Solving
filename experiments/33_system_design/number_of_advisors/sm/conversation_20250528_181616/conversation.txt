LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1\) m
- \(L = 10\) m
- \(v = 0.01\) m/s
- \(\rho \approx 1000\) kg/m³

We need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Reynolds number \(Re < 2000\)), \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3}\) Pa·s, we can calculate \(Re\) first.

Let's calculate \(Re\) and the corresponding \(f\). After that, we can substitute \(f\) back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss.

Team, please confirm the assumptions and calculations for \(Re\) and \(f\), and suggest any additional considerations we might need to take into account.

ADVISOR_1: To confirm the calculations for the Reynolds number \(Re\) and the friction factor \(f\), let’s proceed step-by-step.

1. **Calculate Reynolds number \(Re\)**:

   The formula for Reynolds number is:

   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]

   Substituting the known values:

   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)

   We get:

   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}} = \frac{1 \, \text{kg/m·s}}{1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}} = 1000
   \]

   Since \(Re < 2000\), this confirms that the flow is indeed laminar.

2. **Calculate friction factor \(f\)**:

   For laminar flow, the friction factor is given by:

   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re}
   \]

   Substituting the calculated \(Re\):

   \[
   f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

3. **Substitute \(f\) back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation**:

   The Darcy-Weisbach equation is:

   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]

   Substituting the values:

   - \(f = 0.064\)
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

   We calculate:

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]

   Simplifying this:

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

### Summary of Results:
- Reynolds number \(Re = 1000\) (laminar flow)
- Friction factor \(f = 0.064\)
- Pressure loss \(\Delta P = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}\)
