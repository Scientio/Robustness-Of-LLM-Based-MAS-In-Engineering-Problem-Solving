\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Given the values:
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\) (density of water),
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\) (flow velocity),
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\) (diameter of the pipe),
- \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) (dynamic viscosity of water).

Now, substituting the values into the Reynolds number equation:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \times 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \times 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}} 
\]

Calculating this gives:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Now that we have calculated \(Re = 1000\), we need to determine the flow regime. A Reynolds number less than 2000 typically indicates laminar flow, while a number greater than 4000 indicates turbulent flow. Since \(Re = 1000\), we are in the laminar flow regime.

For laminar flow, the friction factor \(f\) can be calculated using the formula:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Substituting \(Re = 1000\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the known values:

- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\),
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\),
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\),
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\),
- \(f = 0.064\).

Calculating \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each term:

1. \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\),
2. \(\frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\).

Now substituting these values into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is \(0.32 \, \text{Pa}\).

### Summary:

3. **Pressure Loss**: \(\Delta P = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}\).
