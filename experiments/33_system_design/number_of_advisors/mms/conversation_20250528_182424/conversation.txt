LEADER: To address the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m, a length of 10m, and a water flow velocity of 0.01m/s, we need to consider the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the appropriate friction factor \(f\):

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature).

Next, we will look up the friction factor \(f\) based on the Reynolds number. For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \(f\) can be calculated as:

\[ f = \frac{64}{Re} \]

For turbulent flow (Re > 4000), we would use empirical correlations or charts to find \(f\).

Given the low flow velocity, we expect the flow to be laminar. Let's calculate the Reynolds number and subsequently the pressure loss.

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\)**:
   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]
   Substituting the values:
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\) (density of water)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\) (flow velocity)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\) (diameter of the pipe)
   - \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\) (dynamic viscosity of water)

   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = \frac{1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
   \]

2. **Determine the friction factor \(f\)**:
   Since \(Re = 1000\) indicates laminar flow (as \(Re < 2000\)), we can use:
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

3. **Calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\)** using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]
   Substituting the values:
   - \(f = 0.064\)
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \times (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \times 0.0001}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

Therefore, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is **0.32 Pa**.
