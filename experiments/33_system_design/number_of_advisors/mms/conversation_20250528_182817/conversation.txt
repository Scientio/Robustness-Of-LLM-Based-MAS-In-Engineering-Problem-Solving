\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,

- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³),

\[

\]

Assuming water at room temperature, the dynamic viscosity (\(\mu\)) is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\).

Let's calculate \(Re\) and determine if the flow is laminar or turbulent. For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \(f\) can be calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

For turbulent flow, we would use the Moody chart or empirical correlations depending on the pipe's roughness.

Now, I suggest we calculate the Reynolds number and then determine the appropriate friction factor. What do you all think?
