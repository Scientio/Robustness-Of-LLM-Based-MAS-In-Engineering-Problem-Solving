LEADER: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = velocity of the fluid (m/s)

Given:
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(L = 10 \, m\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)
- For water, \(\rho \approx 1000 \, kg/m³\)

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow is slow, we can assume laminar flow if the Reynolds number \(Re < 2000\). The Reynolds number is calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\) at room temperature).

Let’s calculate \(Re\) to determine the flow regime and friction factor \(f\). 

1. Calculate \(Re\).
2. If \(Re < 2000\), use \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\).
3. If \(Re \geq 2000\), we would need to use the Moody chart for turbulent flow, but we will first check if we are in laminar flow.

ADVISOR_3: Let's proceed with the calculations step by step.

### Step 1: Calculate the Reynolds Number \(Re\)

The formula for the Reynolds number is:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Given:
- \(\rho = 1000 \, kg/m³\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(\mu = 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\)

Substituting the values into the equation:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

### Step 2: Determine the Friction Factor \(f\)

Since \(Re < 2000\), we can use the simplified friction factor:

\[
f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

### Step 3: Calculate the Pressure Loss \(\Delta P\)

Now that we have \(f\), we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to calculate the pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the known values:

- \(L = 10 \, m\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(\rho = 1000 \, kg/m³\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

Calculating \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each component:

1. \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
2. \(\frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now substituting these values back into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, Pa
\]

### Conclusion

The pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is \(0.125 \, Pa\).
