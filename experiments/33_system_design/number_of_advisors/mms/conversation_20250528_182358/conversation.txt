LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- Diameter \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- Length \(L = 10 \, m\)
- Flow velocity \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)
- For water, \(\rho \approx 1000 \, kg/m³\)

We need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow velocity is low, we may be dealing with laminar flow. The friction factor for laminar flow is given by:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\), we can calculate \(Re\) and subsequently \(f\).

Team, let's calculate the Reynolds number first to confirm whether we are in the laminar or turbulent regime. After that, we can find the friction factor and then compute the pressure loss. What do you think?

ADVISOR_3: Let's proceed with calculating the Reynolds number \(Re\) using the given parameters:

1. **Parameters**:
   - Diameter \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
   - Flow velocity \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)
   - Density of water \(\rho \approx 1000 \, kg/m³\)
   - Dynamic viscosity of water \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\)

2. **Calculate Reynolds number**:
   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]
   Substituting the values:
   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \, kg/m³ \cdot 0.01 \, m/s \cdot 0.1 \, m}{0.001 \, Pa \cdot s}
   \]
   \[
   Re = \frac{1 \, kg/(m \cdot s)}{0.001 \, Pa \cdot s} = 1000
   \]

3. **Calculate the friction factor**:
   Using the simplified friction factor \(f = \frac{25}{Re}\):
   \[
   f = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
   \]

4. **Calculate the pressure loss using the Darcy-Weisbach equation**:
   Now we can substitute \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10 \, m}{0.1 \, m} \cdot \frac{1000 \, kg/m³ \cdot (0.01 \, m/s)^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{0.1}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 5 = 0.125 \, Pa
   \]

Thus, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is \(0.125 \, Pa\).
