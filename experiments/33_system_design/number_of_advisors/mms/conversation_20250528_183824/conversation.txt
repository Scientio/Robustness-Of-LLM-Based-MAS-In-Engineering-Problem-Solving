LEADER: To our team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

The pressure loss due to friction can be calculated using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature).

After calculating the Reynolds number, we can determine the friction factor \(f\) using the appropriate correlation (e.g., the Moody chart for turbulent flow or the formula for laminar flow).

Given the low flow velocity, we should expect laminar flow, which means we can use:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Let's discuss the calculation of the Reynolds number and the resulting friction factor. What are your thoughts on this approach?

   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
   \]

2. **Calculate the friction factor**:
   Using \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\):
   \[
   f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
   \]

   Alternatively, if we use \(f = \frac{25}{Re}\):
   \[
   f = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
   \]

3. **Calculate the pressure loss**:
   Using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]

   For \(f = 0.064\):
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

   For \(f = 0.025\):
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
