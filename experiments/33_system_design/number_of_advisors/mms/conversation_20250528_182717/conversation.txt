\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\)).

Next, we can find the appropriate \(f\) using the Moody chart or empirical correlations based on the Reynolds number. 

Let's calculate the Reynolds number:

1. Calculate \(Re\).
2. Determine \(f\) based on \(Re\).
3. Substitute \(f\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\).
