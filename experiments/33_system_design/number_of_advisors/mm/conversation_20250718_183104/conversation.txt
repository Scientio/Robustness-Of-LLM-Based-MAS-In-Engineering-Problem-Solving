LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow velocity is low, we may expect laminar flow, which can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) (Reynolds number) is calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number and then the friction factor, followed by the pressure loss.

\]

Substituting the values:

- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Now, using our friction factor assumption:

\[
f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

Now we can substitute \(f\) back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \times (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this gives:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \times 0.0001}{2} = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) is 0.125 Pa. Let me know if you need any further clarifications!
