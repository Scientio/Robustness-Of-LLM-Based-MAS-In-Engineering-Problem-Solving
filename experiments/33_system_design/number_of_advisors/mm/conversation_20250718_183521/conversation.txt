LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we need to use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the velocity of the fluid.

Given:
- \(D = 0.1\) m,
- \(L = 10\) m,
- \(v = 0.01\) m/s.

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to determine the flow regime and subsequently the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(1 \times 10^{-3}\) Pa·s.

Next, we can calculate \(Re\) and find \(f\) using the appropriate correlation (e.g., for laminar flow, \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\), and for turbulent flow, we would use the Moody chart).

Let's calculate \(Re\) and check if the flow is laminar or turbulent. After that, we can determine \(f\) and use it to find \(\Delta P\).
