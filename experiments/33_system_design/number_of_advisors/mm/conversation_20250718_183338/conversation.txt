LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

We need to calculate the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

For water at room temperature, we can approximate:
- \(\rho \approx 1000 \, kg/m³\)
- \(\mu \approx 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\)

Given our parameters:
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(L = 10 \, m\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

Let's calculate \(Re\) first.

I suggest we calculate \(Re\) and then determine \(f\). After that, we can find the pressure loss. What do you think?

Let's calculate \(Re\) using the correct values:

1. Calculate \(Re\):
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, kg/m³\)
   - \(\mu = 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

2. Now, calculate \(f\):
\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. Finally, we can find the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):
\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]
\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, Pa
\]

Preliminary Decision: The pressure loss in the pipe is 0.32 Pa.
