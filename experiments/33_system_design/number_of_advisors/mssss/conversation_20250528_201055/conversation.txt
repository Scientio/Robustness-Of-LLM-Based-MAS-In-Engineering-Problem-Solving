LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \(f\) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \(\rho\) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \(v\) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- Diameter \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- Length \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- Velocity \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
- Assuming water, \(\rho \approx 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to determine the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) for water of approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\):

Next, we can find \(f\) using the appropriate correlation (for laminar or turbulent flow).

Let's proceed to calculate the Reynolds number and subsequently the friction factor. Team, please provide your insights on the calculations and any considerations for determining the friction factor.
