LEADER: To our team, we have a problem involving pressure loss in a pipe with the following specifications: a diameter of 0.1m, a length of 10m, and a water flow velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/mÂ³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (Re < 2000), \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{\text{Re}}
\]

Where Re is the Reynolds number, given by:

\[
\text{Re} = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature, we can calculate Re for our flow conditions.

Let's calculate Re and then determine \(f\). 

What are your thoughts on this approach?

Given the parameters you've provided, let's first calculate the Reynolds number using the standard equation:

\[
\text{Re} = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Substituting in the values:
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\) (density of water),
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\) (flow velocity),
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\) (diameter of the pipe),
- \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) (dynamic viscosity of water).

Calculating Re:

\[
\text{Re} = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

Since \(\text{Re} < 2000\), we are indeed in the laminar flow regime. Thus, we can use the laminar flow friction factor:

\[
f = \frac{64}{\text{Re}} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now we can substitute \(f\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting in the values:
- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\),
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\),
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\),
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\).

Calculating:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately \(0.32 \, \text{Pa}\). 

This approach ensures accuracy by using the correct friction factor for laminar flow. What do you think about this calculation?
