LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\), we can calculate \(Re\).

Next, we can use the appropriate correlation for \(f\) based on the flow regime (laminar or turbulent) determined by \(Re\).

Let's start with the calculations for \(Re\) and then find \(f\). Team, what do you think about this approach? Do you have suggestions or insights on how to calculate \(f\) accurately?

- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)

We can calculate \(Re\) using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Substituting the values:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}}
\]

Calculating this gives:

\[
Re = \frac{1 \, \text{kg/(m·s)}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re = 1000\), which is less than 2000, we are indeed in the laminar flow regime. Now we can use the simplified friction factor:

\[
f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

Now that we have \(f\), we can substitute it back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):

Given:
- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

The Darcy-Weisbach equation is:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the known values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10 \, \text{m}}{0.1 \, \text{m}} \cdot \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot (0.01 \, \text{m/s})^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each part:

1. \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
2. \(\frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now substituting these values back into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]

Calculating:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 5 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is \(0.125 \, \text{Pa}\).
