LEADER: To calculate the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number:

1. Calculate \(Re\).
2. Depending on the \(Re\), determine the appropriate \(f\) using the Moody chart or empirical correlations (for laminar flow \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\) and for turbulent flow use the Colebrook equation).

After finding \(f\), we can substitute it back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss.

However, if we proceed with this approach, we can still quickly calculate the Reynolds number and friction factor. Let's go ahead and calculate the Reynolds number first using the given parameters:

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\)**:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}} 
\]

\[
Re = \frac{1 \, \text{kg/(m·s)}}{1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}} = 1000
\]

2. **Determine the friction factor \(f\)**:

Since \(Re = 1000\) is less than 2000, the flow is laminar. Using \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

3. **Now substitute \(f\) back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \(\Delta P\)**:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10 \, \text{m}}{0.1 \, \text{m}} \cdot \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot (0.01 \, \text{m/s})^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each part:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is **0.32 Pa**.
