LEADER: Team, we need to determine the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, with water flowing at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for pressure loss, which is given by:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \( f \):

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is about \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s} \), we can calculate the Reynolds number and then determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent to find \( f \).

I suggest we start by calculating the Reynolds number and then use the appropriate correlation for \( f \). What do you think?

Given:
- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \) (density of water),

- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \) (diameter of the pipe),

\[ Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000 \]

2. **Determine the friction factor \( f \)**:

Using the simplified relation:
\[ f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025 \]

3. **Calculate the pressure loss \( \Delta P \)**:

Now we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:
\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

Given:
- \( L = 10 \, \text{m} \) (length of the pipe),
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \) (diameter of the pipe),
- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \),
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \).

Substituting the values:
\[ \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} \]
\[ = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} \]
\[ = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 \]
\[ = 0.025 \cdot 5 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa} \]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is approximately **0.125 Pa**. Would you like to discuss further implications or adjustments for this calculation?
