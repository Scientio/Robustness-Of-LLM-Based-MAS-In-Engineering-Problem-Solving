- Density of water \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- Dynamic viscosity of water \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa} \cdot \text{s}\)

The formula for Reynolds number is:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Substituting the values:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa} \cdot \text{s}}
\]

Calculating it step by step:

1. Calculate the numerator: 
   \[
   1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1 = 1 \, \text{kg/m}^2\cdot\text{s}
   \]

2. Now divide by the dynamic viscosity:
   \[
   Re = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000
   \]

Now that we have \(Re = 1000\), we can determine the flow regime. Since \(Re < 2000\), the flow is laminar.

For laminar flow, the friction factor \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Substituting \(Re = 1000\):

\[
f = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

Now we can substitute \(f\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the known values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each part step by step:

1. Calculate \(\frac{L}{D}\):
   \[
   \frac{10}{0.1} = 100
   \]

2. Calculate \(\frac{\rho v^2}{2}\):
   \[
   \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = \frac{0.1}{2} = 0.05
   \]

Now substituting these values into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]

Calculating:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 5 = 0.32 \, \text{Pa}
\]
