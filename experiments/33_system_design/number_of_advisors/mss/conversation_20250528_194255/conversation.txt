LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe due to friction, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ \Delta P = f \cdot \left(\frac{L}{D}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{\rho v^2}{2}\right) \]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

Given:
- Diameter \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- Length \(L = 10 \, m\)
- Flow velocity \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to determine the flow regime and then find the friction factor \(f\).

The Reynolds number \(Re\) is given by:

\[ Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} \]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) for water at room temperature is about \(0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\):

1. Calculate \(Re\).
2. Determine \(f\) based on whether the flow is laminar or turbulent.
3. Use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\).

Given the parameters we have, let's first calculate the Reynolds number to see which regime we are in. 

1. **Calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\)**:

Using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Substituting the values:

- \(\rho = 1000 \, kg/m^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, m/s\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, m\)
- \(\mu = 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s\)

We get:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
\]

2. **Determine the flow regime**:

Since \(Re = 1000\), the flow is laminar (Re < 2000).

3. **Calculate the friction factor \(f\)**:

For laminar flow, we can use the relationship:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re} = \frac{64}{1000} = 0.064
\]

4. **Use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\)**:

Now substituting \(f\), \(L\), \(D\), \(\rho\), and \(v\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \left(\frac{L}{D}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{\rho v^2}{2}\right)
\]

Substituting the values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot \left(\frac{10}{0.1}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}\right)
\]

Calculating step-by-step:

1. \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\)
2. \(\frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05\)

Now substituting these into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.064 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.32 \, Pa
\]

Thus, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) due to friction in the pipe is \(0.32 \, Pa\).
