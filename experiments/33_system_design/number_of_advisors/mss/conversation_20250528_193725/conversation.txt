LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in a pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to determine the flow regime and the appropriate friction factor:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\), we can calculate \(Re\).

Next, we can use the appropriate correlation to find the friction factor \(f\). For laminar flow (\(Re < 2000\)), \(f\) can be calculated as:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

For turbulent flow, we would use the Moody chart or empirical correlations.

Let's calculate \(Re\) and determine \(f\). Team, could you assist with the calculations and confirm the flow regime?
