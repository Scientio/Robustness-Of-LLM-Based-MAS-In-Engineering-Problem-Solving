To find the pressure loss, we need to use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the pipe length,
- \(D\) is the pipe diameter,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m続),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the friction factor \(f\). Given the low flow velocity, we suspect laminar flow, which would mean we can use the formula:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

Where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number, calculated as:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is around \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa.s}\).

Let's calculate \(Re\) and subsequently \(f\). What do you think? Should we proceed with these calculations?

4. **Calculate Pressure Loss \(\Delta P\):**
   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]

   \[

   \]
