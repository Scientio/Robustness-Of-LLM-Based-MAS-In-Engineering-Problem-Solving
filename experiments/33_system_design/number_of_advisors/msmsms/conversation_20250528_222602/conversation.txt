LEADER: To solve the problem of pressure loss in a pipe, we need to apply the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

To find the friction factor \(f\), we need to determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent. The Reynolds number \(Re\) can be calculated as:

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Assuming a dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water around 0.001 Pa·s, we can calculate \(Re\) to determine the flow regime.

Let's calculate \(Re\) first, and then we can identify the friction factor using the appropriate correlations. 

I suggest we proceed with these calculations. Any thoughts on this approach?

Let's proceed with the calculation of the Reynolds number \(Re\) using the provided values:

1. **Given values**:
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\)

2. **Calculate \(Re\)**:
   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = 1000
   \]

Since \(Re = 1000\), which is less than 2000, the flow is indeed laminar. 

3. **Calculate the friction factor \(f\)**:
   \[
   f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
   \]

4. **Now, we can substitute \(f\) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation**:
   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]
   Substituting the values:
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]

5. **Calculating the pressure loss**:
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is \(0.125 \, \text{Pa}\).
