LEADER: Team, we need to determine the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water is flowing at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[ 
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} 
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(0.001 \text{Pa.s}\), we can calculate \(Re\).

Once we have \(Re\), we can determine \(f\) using the appropriate correlations for laminar or turbulent flow. Given the low velocity, we might expect laminar flow, which uses:

\[ 
f = \frac{64}{Re} 
\]

Let's calculate \(Re\) first and then find \(f\). What do you think?

- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\) (velocity of water),
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\) (diameter of the pipe),
- \(\mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) (dynamic viscosity of water).

Substituting these values into the equation:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \times 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \times 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}}

Calculating this:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1}{0.001} = \frac{1}{0.001} = 1000

\[

\]

Now we have \(f = 0.025\). Next, we can substitute this value along with the other known values into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10 \, \text{m}}{0.1 \, \text{m}} \cdot \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot (0.01 \, \text{m/s})^2}{2}
\]

Calculating this step-by-step:

1. Calculate \(\frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100\).
2. Calculate \(v^2 = (0.01)^2 = 0.0001 \, \text{m}^2/\text{s}^2\).
3. Calculate \(\frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = \frac{0.1}{2} = 0.05 \, \text{Pa}\).

Now, substituting these values into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]

Calculating this:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 5 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
\]

So, the pressure loss in the pipe is \(\Delta P = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}\).
