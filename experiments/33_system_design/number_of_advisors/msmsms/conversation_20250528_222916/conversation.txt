LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we need to apply the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid (for water, approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) to determine the flow regime and subsequently find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature).

Given:
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

Let's calculate \(Re\) and then determine \(f\). 

Can anyone provide insights on how to calculate the friction factor \(f\) for this flow condition? Should we use the Moody chart or empirical correlations?

1. **Given Data:**
   - Diameter of the pipe, \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - Length of the pipe, \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - Flow velocity, \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - Density of water, \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - Dynamic viscosity of water, \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)

2. **Calculate Reynolds Number \(Re\):**
   \[
   Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
   \]
   Plugging in the values:
   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}} = \frac{1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
   \]

3. **Calculate the Friction Factor \(f\):**
   Using the simplified relationship:
   \[
   f = \frac{25}{Re}
   \]
   Substituting \(Re = 1000\):
   \[
   f = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
   \]

4. **Now, we can calculate the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:**
   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]
   Substituting the values:
   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10 \, \text{m}}{0.1 \, \text{m}} \cdot \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot (0.01 \, \text{m/s})^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
   = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]
   \[
   = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{0.1}{2}
   \]
   \[
   = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
   \]
   \[
   = 0.025 \cdot 5 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

Thus, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is **0.125 Pa**. If you have any further questions or need additional calculations, feel free to ask!
