\[ \Delta P = f \frac{L}{D} \frac{\rho v^2}{2} \]

- \( \Delta P \) = pressure loss (Pa)
- \( f \) = friction factor (dimensionless)
- \( L \) = length of the pipe (m)
- \( D \) = diameter of the pipe (m)
- \( \rho \) = density of the fluid (kg/m³)
- \( v \) = flow velocity (m/s)

Given:
- Diameter \( D = 0.1 \, m \)
- Length \( L = 10 \, m \)
- Flow velocity \( v = 0.01 \, m/s \)
- Density of water \( \rho \approx 1000 \, kg/m³ \)

ADVISOR_2: Let's calculate the Reynolds number using the provided formula:

\[
\text{Re} = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Given:
- \( \rho = 1000 \, kg/m³ \)
- \( v = 0.01 \, m/s \)
- \( D = 0.1 \, m \)
- \( \mu = 0.001 \, Pa \cdot s \)

Now substituting the values into the Reynolds number formula:

\[
\text{Re} = \frac{1000 \, kg/m³ \times 0.01 \, m/s \times 0.1 \, m}{0.001 \, Pa \cdot s}
\]

Calculating the numerator:

\[
1000 \times 0.01 \times 0.1 = 1 \, kg/(m \cdot s)
\]

Now dividing by the dynamic viscosity:

\[
\text{Re} = \frac{1 \, kg/(m \cdot s)}{0.001 \, Pa \cdot s} = 1000
\]

The Reynolds number is \( \text{Re} = 1000 \).

Since \( \text{Re} < 2000 \), the flow is laminar. Now, we can use the simplified friction factor:

\[
f = \frac{25}{\text{Re}} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

Now, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \( \Delta P \):

\[
\Delta P = f \frac{L}{D} \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the known values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \times \frac{10 \, m}{0.1 \, m} \times \frac{1000 \, kg/m³ \times (0.01 \, m/s)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each part:

1. \( \frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100 \)
2. \( v^2 = (0.01)^2 = 0.0001 \, m²/s² \)
3. \( \frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \times 0.0001}{2} = 0.05 \, Pa \)

Now substituting everything back into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \times 100 \times 0.05
\]

Calculating:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \times 5 = 0.125 \, Pa
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is \( \Delta P = 0.125 \, Pa \).
