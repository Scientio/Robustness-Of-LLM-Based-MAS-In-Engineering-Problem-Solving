LEADER: To our team, we need to calculate the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows at a velocity of 0.01m/s. The pressure loss can be determined using the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

We need to find the friction factor \(f\). Since the flow velocity is low, we should check if the flow is laminar or turbulent. For laminar flow, \(f\) can be calculated using:

\[
f = \frac{64}{Re}
\]

where \(Re\) is the Reynolds number given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature, \(\mu\) (dynamic viscosity) is about \(0.001 \, \text{Pa·s}\).

Let's calculate the Reynolds number first, and then determine the friction factor. 

Team, please advise on any additional considerations or if we should proceed with this approach.

\]

3. **Calculate the pressure loss (\(\Delta P\))**:

Now we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}

Substituting the known values:
