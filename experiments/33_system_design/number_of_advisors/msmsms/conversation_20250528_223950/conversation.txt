LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the appropriate friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\) at room temperature).

Next, we will calculate the friction factor \(f\) based on the flow regime (laminar or turbulent).

Let's discuss:
1. Calculate the Reynolds number.
2. Determine the friction factor \(f\) based on the Reynolds number.
3. Use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss.

What do you think about this approach? Any suggestions or concerns?

   \[

   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)

   \[
   Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = \frac{1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
   \]

2. **Determine the friction factor \(f\)**:
   Since \(Re = 1000\), we can use the relation:
   \[
   f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
   \]

3. **Use the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \(\Delta P\)**:
   \[
   \Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
   \]
   Substituting the values:
   - \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
   - \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
   - \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

   \[
   \Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
   \]
   \[
   = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
   \]
   \[
   = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{0.1}{2}
   \]
   \[
   = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
   \]

Thus, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is \(0.125 \, \text{Pa}\).
