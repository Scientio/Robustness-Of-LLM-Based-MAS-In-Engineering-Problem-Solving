LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which is given by:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10 m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1 m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to determine the flow regime and then find the friction factor \(f\). The Reynolds number \(Re\) is given by:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3} \text{Pa·s}\), we can calculate \(Re\).

Once we have \(Re\), we can determine \(f\) using the appropriate correlation (e.g., the Moody chart or empirical equations).

Let's start with calculating \(Re\) and then proceed to find \(f\). Any thoughts or suggestions on this approach, team?

ADVISOR_2: Using the simplified relationship \(f = \frac{25}{Re}\) is a practical approach for laminar flow, where \(Re < 2000\). Let's first calculate the Reynolds number \(Re\) using the provided values:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Substituting the values:

- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)

Calculating \(Re\):

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}} = \frac{1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

Since \(Re = 1000\), which is less than 2000, this confirms that the flow is laminar, and we can use the simplified friction factor formula:

\[
f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

Now that we have \(f\), we can substitute it back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the known values:

- \(L = 10 \, \text{m}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)

Calculating \(\Delta P\):

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

\[
= 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2}
\]

\[
= 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{0.1}{2}
\]

\[
= 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05
\]

\[
= 0.025 \cdot 5 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) in the pipe is \(0.125 \, \text{Pa}\). If there are any further questions or if anyone would like to verify any part of the calculations, feel free to discuss!
