LEADER: Alright team, we need to determine the pressure loss in a pipe with a diameter of 0.1m and a length of 10m, where water flows through it at a velocity of 0.01m/s. 

To solve this, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation, which states:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \( \Delta P \) is the pressure loss,
- \( f \) is the friction factor,
- \( L \) is the length of the pipe,
- \( D \) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \( \rho \) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \( v \) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to calculate the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \( f \):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \( \mu \) of water is about \( 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \), we can calculate \( Re \) and then determine \( f \) using the appropriate correlation (e.g., Moody chart or empirical formulas).

Let's start by calculating the Reynolds number and friction factor. What do you think?

ADVISOR_2: That's a good simplification! Let's first calculate the Reynolds number \( Re \) using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Given the parameters:
- \( \rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \) (density of water),
- \( v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \) (flow velocity),
- \( D = 0.1 \, \text{m} \) (diameter of the pipe),
- \( \mu = 0.001 \, \text{Pa.s} \) (dynamic viscosity of water).

Substituting these values into the equation:

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot 0.01 \, \text{m/s} \cdot 0.1 \, \text{m}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}} = \frac{1 \, \text{kg/(m.s)}}{0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}} = 1000
\]

Now that we have the Reynolds number \( Re = 1000 \), we can use the relationship provided by ADVISOR_1 to find the friction factor \( f \):

\[
f = \frac{25}{Re} = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

With the friction factor calculated, we can now substitute \( f \), \( L \), \( D \), \( \rho \), and \( v \) into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find the pressure loss \( \Delta P \):

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10 \, \text{m}}{0.1 \, \text{m}} \cdot \frac{1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \cdot (0.01 \, \text{m/s})^2}{2}
\]

Calculating each part:

1. \( \frac{L}{D} = \frac{10}{0.1} = 100 \)
2. \( v^2 = (0.01)^2 = 0.0001 \)
3. \( \frac{\rho v^2}{2} = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.05 \)

Now substituting these values back into the equation:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125 \, \text{Pa}
\]

Thus, the pressure loss in the pipe is \( \Delta P = 0.125 \, \text{Pa} \).
