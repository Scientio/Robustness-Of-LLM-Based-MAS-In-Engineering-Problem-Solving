LEADER: To determine the pressure loss in the pipe, we can use the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the appropriate friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is about \(1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\):

1. Calculate the Reynolds number.
2. Use the Reynolds number to determine the friction factor \(f\) (using the Moody chart or an appropriate correlation).
3. Substitute \(f\) back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\).

Team, can you please help with calculating the Reynolds number and determining the friction factor for this flow condition?

ADVISOR_1: Sure! Let's start by calculating the Reynolds number using the formula:

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Substituting the values:

- \(\rho = 1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3\)
- \(v = 0.01 \, \text{m/s}\)
- \(D = 0.1 \, \text{m}\)
- \(\mu = 1 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{Pa·s}\)

\[
Re = \frac{1000 \cdot 0.01 \cdot 0.1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = \frac{1}{1 \times 10^{-3}} = 1000
\]

Now, using the friction factor \(f\) which we can assume as \(f = \frac{25}{Re}\):

\[
f = \frac{25}{1000} = 0.025
\]

Now, we can substitute \(f\) back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Substituting the values:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot \frac{10}{0.1} \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot (0.01)^2}{2}
\]

Calculating:

\[
\Delta P = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot \frac{1000 \cdot 0.0001}{2} = 0.025 \cdot 100 \cdot 0.05 = 0.125
\]

So, the pressure loss \(\Delta P\) is \(0.125 \, \text{Pa}\).
