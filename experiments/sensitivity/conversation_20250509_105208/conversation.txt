\[ 
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2} 
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) = pressure loss
- \(f\) = friction factor
- \(L\) = length of the pipe (10m)
- \(D\) = diameter of the pipe (0.1m)
- \(\rho\) = density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³)
- \(v\) = velocity of water (0.01 m/s)

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Where \(\mu\) is the dynamic viscosity of water (approximately 0.001 Pa·s). 

Once we have the Reynolds number, we can find \(f\) using the appropriate correlation, either from the Moody chart or empirical formulas for laminar or turbulent flow.
