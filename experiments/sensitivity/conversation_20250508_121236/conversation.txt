\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe (10m),
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe (0.1m),
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity (0.01 m/s).

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) of water is around \(0.001 \, \text{Pa.s}\) at room temperature, we can calculate the Reynolds number.

Once we have the Reynolds number, we can determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent:
- For laminar flow (\(Re < 2000\)), \(f = \frac{64}{Re}\).
- For turbulent flow, we may need to use the Moody chart or empirical formulas.
