We will use the Darcy-Weisbach equation for this calculation:

\[
\Delta P = f \cdot \frac{L}{D} \cdot \frac{\rho v^2}{2}
\]

Where:
- \(\Delta P\) is the pressure loss,
- \(f\) is the friction factor,
- \(L\) is the length of the pipe,
- \(D\) is the diameter of the pipe,
- \(\rho\) is the density of water (approximately 1000 kg/m³),
- \(v\) is the flow velocity.

First, we need to determine the Reynolds number to find the friction factor \(f\):

\[
Re = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu}
\]

Assuming water at room temperature, the dynamic viscosity \(\mu\) is approximately 0.001 Pa·s. 

Let's calculate the Reynolds number:

1. Calculate \(Re\).
2. Determine the friction factor \(f\) based on the flow regime (laminar or turbulent).
3. Substitute \(f\) back into the Darcy-Weisbach equation to find \(\Delta P\).
